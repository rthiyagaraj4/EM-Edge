Body Parts Tab

  1. Enter the search criteria and click [Search] to select a specific body part. Body Part details are displayed based on the entered criteria. The status of the patient is indicated by a unique color code.

    1. Registration No - The mortuary registration number generated on registering the deceased /body part is displayed.

    2. Registration Date/Time - The date and time of registration is displayed.

    3. Deceased Name/Patient Name - The name of the deceased is displayed and hyperlinked in 'Deceased Patient' Tab and the name of the patient whose body part is registered in the mortuary is displayed and hyperlinked in the 'Body Parts' Tab.

    4. Patient ID - The Patient ID generated when the patient was registered in the Master Patient Index module is displayed.

    5. Age - The age of the patient recorded when the patient was registered to the facility is displayed.

    6. Sex - The gender of the patient is displayed.

    7. PM Y/N - If postmortem is required for the deceased/body part, then "Yes" is displayed, else "No" is displayed. The values are displayed based on the Postmortem Required field in Deceased Registration/Body Part Registration function.

    8. Duration in Mortuary - The duration of the deceased/body part in the mortuary is displayed.

    9. Religion - The religion of the deceased/body part is displayed.

    10. Encounter ID - The last encounter ID of the patient is displayed.

    11. Location - The location assigned to the deceased /body part in the area during registration is displayed.

    12. Body Part Type - This is displayed only in the 'Body Parts' Tab. The type of body part registered for the patient is displayed as 'Amputated Body Part/Dead Fetus/Unidentified Body Part'.

    13. Burial Permit No - The Burial Permit Number is displayed if the burial permit was generated for the patient.

    1. Registration number - Enter the registration number or select the registration number using [?] function. The [?] function displays only those registration numbers of bodies for which the 'Exportation Required' field is checked in the "Release Deceased/Body Part" function.

    2. Patient ID - Enter the patient ID in alphanumeric format of 10 characters maximum length or select the deceased patient ID using [?] function. The [?] function displays the patient IDs of those deceased patients for which 'Exportation Required' field is checked in the "Release Deceased/Body Part" function.

    3. Area Type - Release Area is displayed, by default.

    4. Area - Select the area from the list. The listed values are defined in the Area function.

    5. Postmortem Status - Select the status of postmortem from the list. The listed values are 'Not Applicable' and 'Completed'.

    6. Release Status - The value is displayed as "Released" and is disabled, by default.

    7. Date From/To - Enter the date range in dd/mm/yyyy format within which the deceased patients were registered in the mortuary or select the date range from the calendar function. This field is mandatory. The current date is displayed, by default.

    8. Gender - Select the gender from the list.

    9. Burial Permit No - Select the burial permit number using [?] function.

    10. Body Part Type - This field is displayed and disabled as 'Not Applicable' for deceased patients. Select a value from the list for body parts. The listed values are Amputated Body Part, Dead Fetus and Unidentified Body Part.

  2. Click the Patient Name hyperlink to export body parts from the mortuary.

    1. Registration No. - The registration number of the deceased/body part is displayed.

    2. Patient Id - The patient ID of the deceased/body part is displayed.

    3. Name - The name of the deceased/body part is displayed as read only text.

    4. Age - The age of the deceased/body part is displayed as read only text.

    5. Sex - The gender of the deceased/body part is displayed as read only text.

    6. Claimant Accompanying - The name of the claimant accompanying the deceased/body part is displayed as read only text.

    7. Health Officer - Enter the name of the health officer in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length who examines the body before transporting the deceased/ body part to the destination.

    8. Designation - Enter the designation of the health officer in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length.

    9. Health Officer Remarks - Enter the remarks of the health officer in alphanumeric format of 200 characters maximum length.

    10. Destination - Enter the destination as to where the deceased/body part has to be exported in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length. This is a mandatory field.

    11. Exportation Address - Enter the destination address in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length.

    12. Embassy Certificate No - Enter the certificate number of embassy in alphanumeric format of 50 characters maximum length.

    13. Issued By - Enter the name of the person who issued the embassy certificate in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length.

    14. Issued Date/Time - Enter the date or select the date of issue of the embassy certificate using the calendar function. This is a mandatory field.

  3. Click [Record] in the 'Export Deceased/Body Part' screen to export the deceased/body part from the mortuary.
