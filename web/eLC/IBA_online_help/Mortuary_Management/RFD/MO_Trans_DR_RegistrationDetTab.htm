Registration Details Tab

This tab is used to record the details of the deceased to register the deceased in the mortuary.

 

Referral Details

The referral details are displayed only on selecting 'Brought Dead' patients from the search screen.

  1. Referral Type - The referral type of the patient is displayed.

  2. Referral Source - The referral source of the patient as recorded is displayed.

  3. HealthCare Setting Type - The healthcare setting type of the patient is displayed as recorded.

  4. Referred By - The name of the person who referred the patient to the facility is displayed as recorded.

 

Deceased Details

  1. Brought Dead - This field displays either 'Yes' or 'No' indicating whether the patient was brought dead to the facility. If the patient registered in the Accident and Emergency module was brought dead to the facility then this field is displayed as 'Yes', otherwise it is displayed as 'No'.

  2. Religion - The religion is displayed if it was already recorded for the patient or select the religion using the [?] function. The religions are defined in the Master Patient Index module. This is a mandatory field.

  3. Unclaimed Body - Select the check box if the body is not claimed by anyone.

  4. High Risk Body - Select the check box if the body is a high risk body. This field appears selected if it was recorded earlier in the check out functions.

  5. Manner of Death upon Receipt - Mention the manner of death according to the information available upon receipt of the deceased body by selecting one of the values from the drop-down list. The values are available as defined in the Manner of Death function. This is a mandatory field.

  6. Manner of Death  after Postmortem – The manner of death as confirmed after performing postmortem. This field will be in disabled mode. It will display the value once it is selected and saved using the Record Postmortem  function.

Note: This field will be displayed only in the Change Deceased Details screen and will be enabled only after postmortem is completed.

  1. Place of Death - Enter the place of death in alphanumeric format of 100 characters maximum length.

  2. Deceased Date/Time - The date and time when the patient was recorded as deceased is displayed. This information is captured at the time of closing the encounter with disposition type as 'Death' in the Inpatient and Daycare Management/Outpatient Management module, while marking the patient as deceased in the Master Patient Index module, while registering the patient as 'Brought Dead' or checking out a patient as deceased in the Accident and Emergency module.

  3. Body Found At - Enter from where the body was found in alphanumeric format of 100 characters maximum length.

  4. Click [Cause of Death] to record the cause of death of the patient. As many causes can be recorded for the cause of death of the patient. If the cause of death for the patient was already recorded, then these details are displayed on the screen. New causes can be added and the old ones can be removed accordingly.

 

Body Receipt Details

  1. Body Release Date/Time from Source - The released date and time of the deceased body from its location is displayed.

  2. Body Receipt Date/Time - Enter the date/time in dd/mm/yyyy hh:mm as to when the body was received in the mortuary or select the date/time from the calendar function. The date/time entered cannot be less than the time displayed in the 'Deceased Date/Time' field. This is a mandatory field. The system date/time is displayed, by default.

  3. Body Condition - Select the condition of the body from the list. The listed values are defined in the Body Condition function. This is a mandatory field

  4. Identification Marks - Enter any identification marks of the deceased body in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length.

 

MLC Details

  1. Medico Legal Case - Select the check box to register the body part as a Medico legal Case in the mortuary. This field is checked by default if it was recorded earlier for the patient. On selecting the check box, [Police Details] is enabled. The 'Postmortem Type' field is disabled and displayed as Medico Legal.

  2. Brought by Police - Check the box if the body was brought to the facility by the police.

  3. Click [Police Details] to enter the police details which is enabled only if the MLC check box is checked.

    1. Police Report No. - Enter the police report number in alphanumeric format of 20 characters maximum length. The police report number is displayed if it is already entered in Accident and Emergency module. This is a mandatory field.

    2. Station - Enter the name of the station in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length. The police station is displayed if it is already entered in Accident and Emergency module. This is a mandatory field.

    3. Police ID - Enter a unique ID for police in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length. This is a mandatory field.

    4. Police Details - Enter the police details in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length. This is a mandatory field.

    5. Police 61 Received - Select the check box if police 61 is received.

    6. Police 61 Received By - Enter the name of the practitioner who received the police 61 in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length or select the name of the practitioner using [?] function. This field becomes mandatory on checking the 'Police Approval Received' field'.

    7. Police 61 Received Date - Enter the date and time when the police approval was received in dd/mm/yyyy hh:mm format. The date entered cannot be grater than the current system date. This field becomes mandatory on checking the 'Police Approval Received' field.

    8. On entering the police details, click [Close] to save and close the police details window.

 

Postmortem Details

  1. Postmortem Required - Select the check box if postmortem is required for the deceased body. This field is displayed as checked if it was recorded earlier for the patient. On selecting the check box, the Burial Permit Tab disappears and [Consent Form] is enabled.

  2. Click [Consent] to record consent for clinical postmortem. This button is enabled if Postmortem Required field is selected. If MLC and Postmortem fields are selected, this button is disabled.

    1. Consent Given By - Enter the name of the person who gave the consent to conduct clinical postmortem in alphanumeric format of 20 characters maximum length. This is a mandatory field

    2. Relationship with deceased - Select the relationship of the person who gave the consent from the list box. The listed values are populated from the Patient Identity module. This is a mandatory field.

    3. Consent Form - Select the required consent form from the list. The listed values are defined in the "Consent Form Type" function. This is a mandatory field

    4. Description - On selecting the required consent form, the description given while recording the consent form type is displayed. The description cannot be edited.

    5. Consent Form Received By - Enter the name of the practitioner who received the consent form in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length or select the practitioner using [?] function. This is a mandatory field

    6. Consent Form Received Date/Time - Enter the date and time when the consent form was received in dd/mm/yyyy hh:mm format or select the date and time using the calendar function. The date/time entered cannot be less than the date/time entered in the 'Body Receipt Date/Time' field and cannot be greater than the current system date/time. This is a mandatory field

    7. On entering the consent form details, click [Close] to save and close the consent for clinical postmortem window.

  3. Postmortem Type - This field is disabled. If the MLC checkbox is selected, this field displays Medico Legal indicating that the type of postmortem is a Medico Legal postmortem. If the 'Postmortem required' field is selected, this field displays Clinical indicating that the type of the postmortem is a clinical postmortem. If MLC and Postmortem Required fields are selected, this field is displayed as Medico Legal.

 

Assign Area

  1. Service - Select the required service from the list. The default service as selected in the MO Parameter function is displayed here by default. Services are linked to the departments of the logged in facility in Application Masters module. This is a mandatory field.

If there is only one service associated with a Department, it will be displayed by default in the Deceased Registration even if not selected in the Default Service field in the MO Parameter screen.

  1. Area - Select the area from the list. The listed values are based on the value recorded for the 'Postmortem Required' check box. The listed values are populated as defined in the Area function. The validations are explained as follows:

Postmortem Required check box

Applicable Values

Selected

Values listed with area type set as

1. Other Area

2. Postmortem Area

3. Storage Area

Does not populate 'Release Area'

Cleared

Values listed with area type set as

1. Other Area

2. Release Area

3. Storage Area

Does not populate 'Postmortem Area'

  1. Location - Select the location using [?] function. Based on the 'Area' selected, the locations are displayed. If the 'Area' is not selected, this field is disabled. A location can also be reassigned if required, in this case the old location will become vacant and it can be assigned to any other patient.

  2. Assign Date/Time - This field is displayed as the current system date and time on selecting the 'Area'. The date and time is displayed in dd/mm/yyyy hh:mm format indicating when the deceased was assigned to the area. The date/time cannot be less than the date/time entered in the 'Body Receipt Date/Time' field. This field is enabled and becomes mandatory on selecting the 'Area'.

  3. RFID Tag No - Select the Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) tag number using [?] function.

 

Embalm Service

  1. Embalm at Hospital – Select this check box if you want the embalm process of the deceased to be performed at the hospital. A new option “Record Embalm” will be enabled in the pop-up menu options in the deceased patients list of Manage Deceased/Body Part screen. If this check box is cleared, the option will get disabled.

  2. Embalm at Home – Select this check box if you want the embalm process of the deceased to be performed at home.

Note 1. By default, both these check boxes are cleared. You can select only one of these check boxes at a time. If you  select both the check boxes, the error message “Either Embalm at Hospital or Embalm at Home can alone be selected at a time” will be displayed.
2. If you  have selected “Embalm at Hospital”, and the embalm function is not completed, you cannot release the body in Mortuary management, an error message “Body cannot be released as Embalm not completed” will be displayed. You need to  complete or cancel the embalm registration an only then release the body.
