Rhesus

From this function you can Query for the existing Rhesus codes and View the details.

The Rhesus function enables you to query the existing Blood Groups. The two important classifications to describe the blood types in humans are ABO grouping and Rhesus (Rh) factor. Each blood unit comprises of Blood Group code and the Rhesus factor. Rhesus factor is a blood group antigen possessed by Rh-positive people; if an Rh-negative person receives a blood transfusion from an Rh-positive person it can result in hemolysis and anaemia. The term Rhesus (Rh) blood group system refers to the five main Rhesus antigens, such as C, c, D, E and e and the many other less frequent Rhesus antigens. The terms Rhesus factor and Rh factor are equivalent and refer to the Rh D antigen. The Rhesus codes are created in Terminology & Coding Management module.  

Querying for Existing Rhesus Records

From the Masters, double-click the Rhesus function.

The Rhesus screen gets displayed.

  1. Enter the Rhesus factor Code in alphanumeric format of four characters maximum length. The descriptions of the Rhesus codes are displayed.

  2. Click [Execute Query] or press [F8] to search for the existing Rhesus records.
