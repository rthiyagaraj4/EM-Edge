Blood Units Entry

From this function you can Record the blood unit details while adding them to the stock. You can also Associate Antigens, Antibodies and Unit Attributes to a blood unit. You can Query to view the existing records.  

The Blood Units Entry function is used to record the blood unit details when it is added to the stock from other facilities. All the products, which require confirmation of Blood Group and Rhesus, are sent to Lab for screening through this function. All the products, which do not require Blood Group confirmation are directly taken into stock. If the Blood Group Confirmation required check box is selected in the Product Codes function, the blood unit has to be sent to lab for screening. Otherwise, the units go to the stock directly. You can associate Antigens, Antibodies and Unit Attributes to the blood unit.

 

A Pilot Tube Label is printed on successful Blood Units Entry, if the 'Screening Required' check box is selected for the respective product in the 'Product Codes' function. On Clicking [Save], system prints pilot tube labels. The blood units are sent for screening only through the ‘Unit Screening’ function.

If the 'Screening Required' check box is cleared for the respective product in the 'Product Codes' function and the 'Blood Bag Print Indicator' is selected as 'Always Print', then Blood Bag Label is printed on successful Blood Units Entry. On Clicking [Save], system prints Blood Bag Labels and the blood units are directly recorded into BT stock.

Recording Blood Units Entry

From the Transactions > Stock Operations, double-click Blood Units Entry function.

The Blood Units Entry screen gets displayed.

  1. Click [List of Values] to select the Product Code to add the blood units to the stock. <Related Field Description>

  2. Right-click ‘Right Click for user Option’ to select the patient. The Patient Lookup option is displayed. Click Patient Lookup and the Patient Lookup screen gets displayed.

  3. You can also click [Execute Query] or press [F8] to view the unit details.

  4. Click [Unit Attributes] to associate the Unit Attributes to the blood unit. Click [Close] to close the screen.

  5. Click [Antigens] to associate Antigens to the selected blood unit. Click [Close] to close the screen.

  6. Click [Next Entry] to refresh the details recorded under Unit Number tab and record next unit number details.

  7. Click 'Antibodies' tab to associate antibodies to the selected patient record.

    Enter the following details:

    1. Antibody Code – Click [List of Values] to select the Antibody Code. The list displays the values defined in the ‘Antibodies’ function.

    2. Description – The Description for the selected Antibody Code is displayed.

  8. Click [Save] to record the Blood Units Entry or [Clear Record] to clear the entered details.

  9. Click [Execute Query] or press [F8] to view the existing records.
