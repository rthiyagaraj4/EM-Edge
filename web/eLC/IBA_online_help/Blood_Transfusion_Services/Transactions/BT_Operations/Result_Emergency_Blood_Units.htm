Result Emergency Blood Units

From this function you can Record crossmatch test results for the units that are issued  in emergency and Print the unit reports of the blood units issued in emergency. You can Query to View the details.

The Result Emergency Blood Units function is used to record the crossmatch test results of the blood units issued in emergency. All the Specimens that have one or more issued Units are queried and resulted in this function. The color code defined at the bottom of the screen describes the status of the specimen selected for the processing. The color codes displayed at the bottom of the screen denotes the status of blood units.

– Units issued in emergency

– Autologous Unit

– Stat (to be issued immediately)

– Urgent

– Directed Unit

– Routine

– Multi allocation Unit

You cannot process cancelled requests.

Resulting Emergency Blood Units

From the Transactions > BT Operations, double-click Result Emergency Blood Units function.

The Result Emergency Blood Units screen gets displayed.

  1. Click [List of Values] to select the specimen number to crossmatch the blood units.

  2. Click [Execute Query] to view the patient details, Product Category and Unit details. <Related Field Description>

  3. Right click on the link Right Click for User Option to select the user. The link has two pop-ups, namely, Patient Chart and Patient Specimen History. Click Patient Chart option to open the Patient Chart Screen from Clinician Access or Patient Specimen History to open the Lab screen from the Laboratory module.

  4. Click [View/Record X Match Results] to enter the cross match test results and validate the result by invoking Cross Match Tests. <Related Field Description>

  5. Click [Details] to view the patient details.

  6. Click [Antibodies] to view the recorded antibodies for the patient. The [Antibodies] gets enabled if antibodies are recorded for the selected patient.

  7. Click [Transfusion History] to view the previous transfusion details of the patient, if any.

  8. Click [Requirements] to view the transfusion requirements recorded for the patient, if any.

  9. Click [View Multi-Allocation] to view the units allocated for multiple specimens. Click [Close] to close the screen.

  10. Click [Product/Category Unit Details] to view category and product details of the unit. Click [Close] to close the screen.

  11. Click [Print] to print the report of the unit report. You can print reports of the blood units that are in Allocated/Compatible/Suitable/Issued/Transfused statuses separately or All the statuses together.  

  12. A report is generated listing patient details, product/category details and unit details.

  13. Click [Commit] to save the transaction. A confirmation message is displayed. Click [OK].
