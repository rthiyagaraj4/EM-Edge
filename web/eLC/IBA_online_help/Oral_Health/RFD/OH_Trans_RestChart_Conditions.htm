Condition

You can use the Condition tab to record a condition against a tooth, tooth range, arch or a quadrant. Based on the Condition set, the external treatment can be recorded as well. The dental conditions enable you to record the orthodontic condition for a selected tooth. The recorded condition can be cancelled or can be marked as an error, so that, no treatment is recorded against the tooth.

You can record several conditions using the Dental Chart or the Data Entry Area.

  1. Click Conditions tab to record the dental conditions of the patient. You can record the condition on a quadrant, arch, a range of tooth, crown, root or on a tooth using the dental chart or the data entry area. You can also mark a recorded condition as an error. The Condition Types differ for a quadrant, arch, tooth, root or crown. The conditions that can be recorded on the relevant surface only are listed.

    The following Condition Types are displayed for Upper or Lower Arch:

    1. Attrition, Abrasion and Erosion

    2. Mal Occlusion

    3. Traumatic Tooth

    The following Condition Types are displayed for Upper Right, Lower Right, Upper Left and Lower Right Quadrants:

    1. Attrition, Abrasion and Erosion

    2. Traumatic Tooth

  2. Click Ext. Treatments to record the dental treatments availed by the patient from the external clinics. You can record the external treatments using the dental chart or the data entry area. You can also select to record the external treatment as a ‘Success’ or ‘Failure’ based on the outcome.

    You can also record the external treatment against a tooth condition, which indicates the treatments performed on the tooth initially at external locations. The details about the external treatment availed can be recorded on the root, crown or the surface of the tooth. The external can also be marked on an arch or a quadrant, if the treatment was availed for an arch or quadrant. On recording the data, the appropriate tooth images are marked in black indicating treatments provided externally. The images for treatment are displayed over root - if treatment was provided on root level, over surface - if treatment was provided on surface level and above the root – if treatment was provided on tooth level. The Category, Site and the Tooth Number fields are populated with the values and are disabled. You can only enter the values in the other fields to record the external treatment for a patient. The external treatment recorded against a tooth is displayed in green lines.

  3. Click Gingival Status tab to record the gingival status details.

  4. Click adjacent to the ‘ Conditions’ menu to view the conditions recorded for the patient.

    The Conditions recorded using the Condition tab are displayed on the right hand side of the screen. You can cancel the condition by marking the condition as an error. Click the condition, which is set as a hyperlink with date and the Condition name. On clicking the hyperlink, the Dental Condition tab is enabled and the recorded data is populated in the respective fields with the field entry being disabled. The Status field is only enabled and you can select the status and save the record.

     

    Enter the following details to mark the condition as an error:

    1. Status – Select a Status from the list. When ‘Marked Error’ is selected, the recorded condition is cancelled and you can record a new condition against the same tooth.

    2. Click [Record] to record the status for the selected Condition or [Reset] to reset and record a new condition. On saving the record with status as ‘Mark Error’, the condition is cancelled and it is not listed on the right hand side menu. The images on the tooth also are deleted.

  5. Click  to view the Ext. Treatments recorded using the External Treatment tab.

  6. Click to view the details recorded using the Gingival Status tab.

  7. Click to view a summary of the Gingival Status and the Periodontal Status recorded for the respective patient. A Dentist can record the Gingival Status on the Sextant or the Quadrant, based on the value set for ‘Gingival Status Recording’ in the ‘Setup Parameters for Facility’ function. The values recorded against the Sextant or the Quadrant get displayed. The Gingival Status Summary and Periodontal Summary options are displayed as hyperlinks. If there is no data recorded, then the options 'Gingival Status' and 'Periodontal Status' are displayed as normal text and clicking is disabled.

    The Periodontal Chart created for a patient using Periodontal Charting under the Charting menu in the Patient Chart gets listed. You can search and view the summary of a specific Evaluation and Baseline Chart. Click the Periodontal Summary hyperlink to view the Periodontal summary.

    Enter the following to view the Summary:

    1. Chart – Select a Chart to view the summary. The Baseline and the Evaluation Charts, which are complete, gets listed.

    2. [Search] – Click [Search] to view the summary of the selected Periodontal Chart.

    The number of conditions recorded indicates the number of tooth against which the gingival status is recorded. The Sum and the Average of the respective Gingival Status is recorded and computed by the system.

    The Gingival Status recorded for a patient using the Gingival Status tab under the Condition tab gets listed. The numbers assigned for each Gingival Status are displayed as a hyperlink. The tooth numbers for which the Gingival Status is recorded gets displayed below the respective Gingival Status number. The tooth number, condition type and the gingival status recorded against the tooth are displayed along with the value.

    To view the summary for a selected gingival status, click the respective gingival status number, which is displayed as a hyperlink. The number of conditions recorded indicates the number of tooth against which the gingival status is recorded. The Sum and the Average of the respective Gingival Status is recorded and computed by the system.

  8. Click to view or add new Baseline Charts to record conditions and treatments for the patient. The Baseline Charts, which are closed and open, are displayed under the respective status. If the chart is for a patient in the deciduous age range, the chart is displayed with the status as [D].

    The Baseline Charts created for a relevant patient are listed under the Baseline Charts menu on the right hand side. Click the date, which is set as a hyperlink to view the chart created on the respective date. You cannot update the Baseline Chart details created already, but only view the details. Click Add/New to create a new Baseline chart.

    A message gets displayed ‘Current Chart will be closed. Do you want to continue creating a New Chart?’ Click [OK] to create a new chart and close the existing chart of the patient. Click [Cancel] to record the conditions and the treatments in the existing chart.

    The current chart is closed on clicking [OK]. The closed charts are displayed under the Baseline Charts with the date made hyperlink. Click the hyperlink to select the closed chart. You cannot record the conditions and the treatments in the closed chart.

  9. Click to view or record Diagnosis details, Clinical Notes, Progress Notes for the patient encounter. Click Dentist Comments hyperlink to record comments in alphanumeric format of 1000 characters maximum length. You can record comments only on a active chart. Click Maxilla Facial Details to view the details that is recorded for the selected patient.

    You can view the conditions and external treatments that are recorded. Conditions include the condition type that is selected, condition, part, area, site, tooth number, name of the user and the date when the condition is recorded. Click [Close] to exit from the screen.

  10. Any external treatments that are included like Treatment Category, external treatments, tooth number, remarks, name of the user and the date when the condition is recorded are displayed.

Progress Notes is enabled only if the Application Task is associated to the responsibility in Clinician Access module.
