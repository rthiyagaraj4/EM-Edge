Blood Groups

From this function you can Query for the existing Blood Groups and View the details.

The Blood Groups function enables you to query for the existing blood groups. Blood is composed of cells suspended in a liquid. The liquid portion is the plasma, from which therapeutic fractions and derivatives are made. Suspended in the plasma are three types of cells, which are the Red cells that carry oxygen, White cells that fight infection and the Platelets that stop bleeding in injuries. The most common type of grouping is the ABO grouping. Red Blood Cells have a protein coat on their surface, which distinguishes them. According to this grouping, blood is divided into four groups such as A, B, AB and O. A rare blood type is any blood type that is difficult to find. For example, AB +ve, O -ve, and A1 -ve are rare types. The blood groups are created in Terminology & Coding Management module.

Querying for Existing Blood Groups

From the Masters, double-click the Blood Groups function.

The Blood Groups screen gets displayed.

  1. Enter the Blood Group Code in alphanumeric format of four characters maximum length. The descriptions of the Blood Group are displayed.

  2. The Indeterminate? check box is selected, only for one particular blood group. This Blood Group is used when the blood group of the donor is not known.

  3. Click [Execute Query] or press [F8] to query for all the existing blood groups.
