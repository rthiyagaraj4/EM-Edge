View Weekly/Monthly Medication administration details

From this function you can View the weekly/monthly medication administration details for a patient.

This function helps in viewing the weekly/monthly medication administration details of the patient. You can also perform functions like Admin, Self Admin, Hold, Could not admin and Discontinue.

View weekly/monthly medication administration details

From the CA Transactions > Clinician Access > Desktop Menu > select required patient > View Chart Summary > select Medication Administration icon

The Medication Administration screen gets displayed.

  1. Select View By as ‘Weekly’ to view the weekly medication administration details of the patient. The system displays the current week by default for administering the drug. You can record medication administration details of the patient only on “Weekly” basis. Or  Select View By as ‘Monthly’ to view the monthly medication administration details. You cannot record any details in “Monthly” basis but only view the details.

From date and To date is read-only. Start date of the week would be Monday

  1. Click More criteria to view additional search criteria. The system displays additional fields such as Display Order By, Admin Route Category, Route of Administration, Include and Display Held/Discontinued Orders.

  2. Select the search criteria as required and click Search for the medication administration details that match the search criteria. You can only view the Bed no, Patient ID, Patient Name, Gender, Age and Nationality.

  3. Scroll down using the scroll bar to view the following links:

    • The text ‘Known Case of Allergy’ is displayed in red when a drug allergy is recorded in the patient record.
    • Click ‘Allergies’ hyperlink to view the Allergy details of the selected patient in the Allergies List dialog.
    • Click ‘Diagnosis’ hyperlink to view the medical problems of the selected patient in the Problem List dialog.
    • Click ‘Current Rx’ hyperlink to view the current medications taken by the patient in the Current Medication dialog.
    • Click ‘Demographics’ hyperlink to view demographics details recorded during patient registration in the Patient Details screen.
    • Click ‘Drug Profile’ hyperlink to view the complete Drug Profile of the selected patient in the Patient Drug Profile screen.

  1. In the Grid, you can view the medication administration details of the patient.

    • Order Date/Time – Displays the Start/End date and time for the medication administration details.

    • Medication Name - Displays the drug name. Click   to view the drug details in the Drug Detail dialog. Click   to drug information and patient education in a separate <drug name> window.

    • Route – Displays  the route of administering the drug to the patient. For example, Oral, Capsule and so on.

    • Freq. – Frequency of administering the drug to the patient. For example, Once a day, Noon

    • Order Dosage – Dosage of drug to be administered to the patient. For example, 10 Tablets.

    • Schedule Time – Time scheduled or fixed by the Doctor for prescribing the drug to the patient.

    • <Date/Day> - Displays the Date and Day of the week as read-only text.

    • <Scheduled Time> hyperlink – Displays the scheduled time as a hyperlink for each day of the week.

      9. Expiry Date – Is enabled and you can select the date using the calendar, if the prescribed drug is a “floor stock” drug.

        1. Drug name – Name of the drug is displayed, by default.

        3. Administered by - Enter the name of the person who administered the drug. This is a mandatory field.

        4. Witnessed by - Enter the name of the person who witnessed the drug administration. This is a mandatory field.

        5. PIN - Enter the Pin number. This is a mandatory field.

        6. OK - Saves the administration details.

  2. To administer a Split Dose medication to the patient, click on the scheduled time against each split dose based on the drug schedule and dosage selected.

  3. To administer IV Drugs with a compatible fluid or only the IV Fluid, click the scheduled time; displays the following options:

    • Start

    • Administer

  1. Click Start to start the IV fluid administration; displays the Start MFR dialog.

Displays the fluids’s name and Total volume in Medication name column. For IV Rx, displays the infusion rate of fluids and Infuse Rate in Admin dose/details column.

  1. Change the Flow Rate, as required.

Displays the Infusion Rate, Batch ID and Expiry date defaulted on the allocated batch.

  1. For floor stock, the Batch ID field will be enabled and remains blank. You can click [?] and select the required batch from Batch search dialog.  

  2. Enter the mandatory details and click OK; the system changes the status to “Started”.

  3. Once the IV administration is started, click the “Schedule time” hyperlink and then select the respective functions as given below:

    • Hold - Displays the Hold MFR dialog in which you can enter the Hold Remarks for holding the medication administration. The system displays the Drug name as read-only. Select the name of the person who is holding the medication in Hold by and Hold Date/Time from the calendar function. Click Ok and the system changes the status to ‘Hold’ upon saving the entered remarks. The record background will get highlighted in black colour where the recorded time will display in white font.

    • Resume – Click on the scheduled medication that is put on hold and then click Resume, the system displays Resume MFR dialog. The system displays the Resume and Complete options. You can also click Complete at this stage without resuming. In the Resume MFR dialog, the system displays the Drug name as read-only. Change the Flow rate if required. Enter the Resume Remarks. Select the name of the person who is holding the medication in Hold by and Hold Date/Time from the calendar function. Click Ok. Once IV Fluids are resumed, the status will be changed to “RESUMED”

    • Complete – If you want to complete the flow after holding the order, you can complete by clicking on “Complete”. Upon clicking, the system displays the Complete MFR dialog. The system displays the current date/time in Complete Date/Time field. The system displays the Drug Name, Batch ID, Expiry Date and Complete By as read-only based on the parameter level setup value “IV Completion Date Time”.

    • Change Flow Rate - You can change the flow rate by clicking Change Flow Rate. The system displays Change Flow Rate MFR dialog which displays the current flow in “Flow Rate From”. You can enter the desired flow rate in Flow Rate To field. The Remarks, Change by and Date fields are displayed as read-only. Click OK, the status changes to “Started” with the changed flow rate in Admin dose/infusion rate.

  1. Once the infusion of IV Fluids are completed, the system will automatically change admin status to “Administered”. Click Administer; the system displays the Medication Administration screen.  

  2. In the Medication Administraton screen, Admin Dtls field, click Dtl to view the Administration Details dialog and enter the administration details such as Remarks, Administered By, Next Administration Date/Time or click the Audit Log hyperlink to view the audit log for IV fluids.

    4. Witnessed by - Enter the name of the person who witnessed the drug administration. This is a mandatory field.

    5. PIN - Enter the Pin number. This is a mandatory field.

    6. OK - Saves the administration details.
