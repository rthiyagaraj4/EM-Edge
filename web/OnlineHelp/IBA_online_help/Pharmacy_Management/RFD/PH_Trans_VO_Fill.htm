Inpatient Category requires additional details and is explained in this section. Filling for outpatient is performed either before the allocation or after it. Refer to the table given for more details.

In the Outpatient and Inpatient Allocate and Fill stages:

  • If the orders are allocated in the Allocate stage, you can modify the allocated prescription in the Fill stage if the billing is not settled.

  • If the orders are filled in the Fill stage , you can modify the filled prescription in the Allocate stage if the billing is not settled.

Filling Orders

Fill for Outpatient

Fill for Outpatient: Fill before Allocation

  1. Click Medical Item hyperlink to allocate a Medical Item with the prescribed Drug.

    1. Enter the Item Name in alphanumeric format of 60 characters maximum length or select the required Item Name using [?] function. This is a mandatory field.

    2. On selecting the Item, the Item details are displayed.

    3. Select the required Batch ID using [?] function.

    4. Enter the Allocated Quantity in numeric format of five digits maximum length. The Charge Amount and Patient Payable Amount is displayed based on the Allocated Quantity.

    5. Click [Record] to record the Item details.

  2. Click [Fill] to invoke Fill/Refill screen.

  3. On filling the required batches and quantities, the details are displayed in the Prescription details frame.

  4. Click Trades hyperlink to view the Allocated Trade/Batch details.

  5. Select the UOM from the 'Issue UOM' or 'Drug UOM' list. This legend is displayed based on the 'Dispense By' value selected in the 'Dispensing Rules' tab of the PH Parameter function.

  6. Click Edit Label hyperlink to enter the instructions.

    Enter the following details:

    1. Print – Select this checkbox to print the pharmacy items. By default, this checkbox is selected.

    2. Patient Details – Patient ID, Patient Name, Prescription Date and Prescribing Practitioner details get displayed.

    3. Drug/Dosage Details – Prescribed Drug, Prescribed Quantity (stock unit) and Expiry Date of the selected batch are displayed. The batch expired date gets displayed only for prescribed drug.

    4. Auxiliary Instructions (English) – Select the Cautionary instructions and special instructions from the list.

    5. Enter the patient instructions in alphanumeric format.

    6. The drug indications entered in the PH Drug master or the drug indications modified during prescription is displayed in the Drug Indications by default.

    7. Click [Copy] to copy the drug remarks to the Patient Instructions

    1. Auxiliary Instructions (Local Language) – Enter the Cautionary instructions, special instructions, patient instructions and drug indications in the required format.

    2. Click [Copy] to copy the drug remarks to the Patient Instructions.

    On entering the required details, click [Print] button, the selected items are printed. click [OK] to record the instructions, [Reset] to clear the entered value or [Cancel] to exit from the Edit Label screen. The ‘Edit Label’ hyperlink gets displayed with a unique color. Once the item is printed, an icon “Printed”   is displayed next to the Patient ID. If you move mouse over the icon, the tool tip “Printed” is displayed. If all the items for the patient are printed, then the “Fully printed icon is displayed. If the entire item is not printed, “Partially Printed  icon is displayed.

  7. Click Findings hyperlink to record the Deliver Stage finding details.

    Enter the Filling Stage Findings details:

    1. Findings – Select the Findings from the list. This is a mandatory field.

    2. Remarks – Enter the Remarks in alphanumeric format. This is a mandatory field.

    On entering the required details, click [OK] to record the findings. The ‘Findings’ hyperlink gets displayed with a unique color-code.

  8. Click Detail hyperlink to view the Fill/Refill details.

  9. Click [Complete Fill].

  1. Click [OK] to invoke the Report screen. The Dispensing Sheet, Dispense Label and Medication Plan Sheet can be generated. These reports get displayed based on the Parameter for Facility setup. Click [OK] for generation of reports. The fill process completes for the selected patient.

Fill For Inpatient

The Dispense Medication login screen for Inpatient category and fill stage. Fill for Inpatients are performed against fill list or without fill list.

To perform Inpatient fill, enter the following details:

1. Dispense Stage – Select FILL stage from the list. This is a mandatory field.

2. Fill – Select either ‘Against Fill List’ or ‘Without Fill List’.

 

Fill for Inpatient: Against Fill List

Inpatient fill list is generated in the function ‘IP Fill Process’. The system automatically allocates batches to the prescribed drugs routed to a selected Dispense Location. Fill process is performed against a fill list. Allocation of drugs is performed automatically by the system and the Pharmacist performs fill only. Fill list is generated for nursing units in the function ‘IP Fill Process’.

 

Fill process is performed without a fill list. The fill performed is similar to outpatient fill. On selecting the required fill option, the screen as shown below gets displayed.

For the medications with Fill ID generated, a new field 'Order Facility' is introduced. Select the Ordering Facility from the list. The legend 'Facility' is changed to Ordering Facility in the 'Current Medication' and 'Pending Orders' screen. The 'Ordering Location' and 'Performing Location' is changed to the respective Facility name.

 

Fill for Inpatient: Without Fill List

• IP Fill without fill list - Prepare Admixture

Select the Order Type as ‘IV with Additives (Admix)’ to Prepare Admixture.

  • Pharmacists prepare admixture for IV/Cyto/TPN orders placed during the fill process. Admixture IV orders routed to the Dispense Location are selected and admixture is prepared in the Dispense Location. The ingredients (drugs) and fluid are mixed in the prescribed proportion during this process.

  • For Inpatient, admixture is prepared during Fill stage. For Outpatients, it is performed either at Allocation or Fill stage based on the Dispense Location setup. On preparing the admixture, the medication is dispensed from the location.

  • Preparation of Admixture is also referred to as Manufacturing transaction.

  • A Worksheet aids the Pharmacist during admixture preparation. Using this sheet, required quantities of the ordered ingredients are drawn from the stock and quantity of reconstitute fluid required for mixing with drug (whose physical form is POWDER) is specified. On stipulating the required quantities, the worksheet is recorded.

  • Once all the ingredients are allocated, the admixture is prepared and transaction finalized. On finalizing, Manufacturing Label and Worksheet are generated. The Manufacturing Label provides information on the Prepared Admixture, Infusion Rate, Infusion Period and Order Number.

  • Select the required Dispense Location, Dispense Stage as ‘Fill’ and fill as ‘Without Fill List’. The Dispense Medication screen gets displayed. Specify the required conditions and select Order Type as ‘IV-with Additives’ to get the Patient ID's displayed. On selecting the required Patient ID, the following details get displayed.

 

• Fill: Without Fill List (TPN Worksheet)

Select the Order Type as ‘TPN Admixture’ to prepare TPN Admixture.

  • When an Inpatient requires administration of Total Parenteral Nutrition, the practitioner orders either a Standard Regimen or a non-standard regimen. Orders of standard regimen (that is Pre-mixed bag supplied by the vendors), are dispensed by the Pharmacist directly. An order for TPN Non-standard Regimen specifies the list of Constituent consisting of Carbohydrate, Lipid, Protein, Electrolytes and Trace Elements and so on. For such orders, it is required that the mixture is specially prepared using the specified constituents and then dispensed from the Pharmacy. The quantity of the individual constituents required for preparation has to be calculated based on the order. In the order, the electrolytes are specified individually as Sodium, Potassium and Acetate. In the Pharmacy, these items are available as Salt, for example, Sodium Chloride, Sodium Acetate, etc. The Pharmacist, who prepares the mixture, draws Sodium from Sodium Chloride or Sodium Acetate or Both, depending on the Order placed by the doctor. For this reason, the molecular formula of the particular salt is used for calculation. For enabling the Pharmacist to calculate the required quantity of the individual constituents for preparing the mixture, the worksheet is used. While preparing TPN admixture it is mandatory for the Pharmacist to use the worksheet. The system does not allow the individual elements to be dispensed.

  • Usage of worksheet for 'Outpatient' dispensing

  • The Outpatient dispensing process consists of various stages namely Registration, Verify, Fill, Allocate, Bill/Receipt and Deliver. The TPN worksheet is used either during Fill or Allocate stage. If the Fill Process is applicable to a Dispense Location and Fill is to be performed before Allocation, the user uses the worksheet as part of the Fill Process and not during the 'Allocate' stage. If 'Fill' is 'After Allocation’, you can use the worksheet as part of 'Allocate' stage and not during the 'Fill' stage. If the Fill Process is not applicable to a Dispense Location, the user uses the worksheet during 'Allocate' stage.

  • On specifying the values in Dispense Medication login screen for Inpatient category and Filling Dispense Stage. The Dispense Medication screen gets displayed. Specify the required query conditions and select ‘TPN Admixture’ as Order Type.
