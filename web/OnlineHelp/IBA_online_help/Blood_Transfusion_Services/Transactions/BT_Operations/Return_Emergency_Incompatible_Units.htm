Return Emergency Incompatible Units

From this function you can Return the incompatible blood units that are issued in emergency and Print the Unit details.

The Return Emergency Incompatible Units function allows you to return the incompatible units issued in emergency. You can print the Unit details of a selected specimen. You cannot process requests that are cancelled. You can view the patient details, antibodies, transfusion details, transfusion requirements, Xmatch Results, Multi-Allocation details, category and product details, Attributes and Antigens. The color codes displayed at the bottom of the screen denote the following.

– Units issued in emergency

– Autologous Unit

– Stat (to be issued immediately)

– Urgent

– Directed Unit

– Routine

– Multi allocation Unit

Return Emergency Incompatible Units

From the Transactions > BT Operations, double-click Return Emergency Incompatible Units function.

The Return Emergency Incompatible Units screen gets displayed.

  1. Click [List of Values] to select the specimen number to return the incompatible blood unit.

  2. Click [Execute Query] to view the patient details, product category and unit details.<Related Field Description>

  3. Click [Details] to view the patient details.  

  4. Click [Antibodies] to view the antibodies recorded for the patient. The [Antibodies] gets enabled if antibodies are recorded for the selected patient.

  5. Click [Transfusion History] to view the previous transfusion details of the patient, if any.

  6. Click [Requirements] to view the transfusion requirements recorded for the patient, if any.

  7. Click [Mother’s Specimen Details] to view the Mother’s Specimen Details. The button is displayed if the mother specimen details are recorded while requesting for the blood units.

  8. Click [View Xmatch Results] to invoke Cross Match Tests screen to view cross match results. Click [OK] to close the Cross Match Tests screen.

  9. Click [View Multi-Allocation] to view the units allocated for multiple specimens. Click [Close] to close the screen.

  10. Click [Product/Category Unit Details] to view category and product details of the unit. Click [Close] to close the screen.

  11. Click [Unit Attributes] to view the Attributes of the patients.

  12. Click [Unit Antigens] to view the Antigens of the patients.

  13. Click [Revert Issue] to revert the issued units.

    The following details are displayed:

    1. Issued By – The name of the user who has issued the blood is displayed.

    2. Issued Date - The Date when the blood unit was issued is displayed.

    3. Issued To - The name of the patient to whom the blood unit was issued is displayed.

    4. Reverted By – Click [List of Values] to select the name of the user who reverted the blood.

    5. Reverted Date – Click the Calendar function to select the date when the blood unit is reverted.

    6. Storage Location – Click [List of Values] to select the storage location, where the selected blood will be stored.

    7. [Save & close] – Click [Save & close] to save the transaction.

    8. [Cancel] - Click [Cancel] to cancel the transaction.

  14. Click [Return] to return the selected incompatible unit issued in emergency. If the blood unit is outside the blood bank for more than a specified period the blood unit gets spoiled and you cannot return it back to the blood bank. <Related Field Description>

  15. Click [Save & Close] to return the blood unit or [Cancel] to cancel the process.

  16. Click [Print] to print unit report. You can print unit reports for allocated, compatible/suitable, issued, transfused units or all Units together. A report is generated with the details.
