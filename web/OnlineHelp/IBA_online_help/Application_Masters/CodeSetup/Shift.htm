Shift

From this function you can Create various shifts applicable for the enterprise and Query for a required Shift record.

Shift timings can overlap if set in parameters and any number of shifts can be created using this master.

Shifts can be identified as Productive and Un-Productive.

• Productive shifts refer to those shifts in which staffs do productive work in the facility.

• Un-Productive shifts are those shifts in which staffs are not currently present in the facility during shift hours, but they are working for the facility and can be available in case of emergency. In other words, staffs are on official absence during Un-Productive shifts.

Productive shifts can further be classified as Working and On-Call.

• Working shifts are those shifts in which, the staffs are scheduled to work for the facility and are physically present in the facility during shift hours.

• On-call shifts are those shifts in which, staffs are scheduled to work for the facility but need not be physically present in the facility. Staffs can be available immediately in case of an emergency.

Apart from this, shifts can be applicable to Working days, Non-Working days and Holidays.

• Working days are the normal working days of the facility.

• Non-working days are the standard predefined days that are non-working for the facility, for example, Sunday.

• Holidays are those days, which are declared as holiday due to certain occasions and which can fall on any working days other than the standard non-working days of the facility.

Creating a Shift

From the Shift Setup, select the Shift function. The Shift screen gets displayed.

  1. Click [Create] to invoke the Create screen.

  2. Enter the details in the mandatory (*) fields. <Related Field Descriptions>

  3. Click [Apply] to save a defined shift or [Reset] to clear the entered data.

  4. Click [Query] to search for an existing shift.
