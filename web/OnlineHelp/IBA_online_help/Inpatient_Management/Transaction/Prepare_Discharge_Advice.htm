Prepare Discharge Advice

From this function you can Prepare the Discharge Advice for an inpatient and notify the discharge desk that a patient is ready for discharge.

The discharge details are entered and discharge notification slip is printed using the function. This function lists out all the Inpatients of the facility. They can be Inpatients whose care in the facility is completed, Inpatients who have absconded, the Inpatients who have been sent on leave and have not yet returned after the expiry of their leave period or Inpatients who have been granted leave and do not want to come back after the leave (No show). The Status for each Inpatient is displayed by an appropriate color code. The legend for each color code is displayed at the bottom of the screen. Prepare Discharge Advice function generates a Discharge Advice for the Inpatient. Only those Inpatients with discharge advice prepared are discharged. Discharge Advice cannot be prepared for unassigned (patients for whom there is no bed assigned) patients.

 

If you have selected the Enable Initiate Pre-Discharge check box in the IP Parameter screen, unless the practitioner initiates the pre-discharge activity, the nurses cannot prepare the discharge advice; an error message “Pre-discharge not initiated; cannot proceed” will be displayed.

Preparing Discharge Advice

From the IP Transactions > Discharge, select the Prepare Discharge Advice function.

The Prepare Discharge Advice screen gets displayed.

  1. Click [Search] to display the list of all the Inpatients or enter the search criteria and click [Search] to select a specific Inpatient. Click [Clear] to reset the records displayed in the result area.

    Enter the following query criteria:

    1. Nursing Unit – Enter the Nursing Unit in alphanumeric format of 12 characters maximum length or select the Nursing Unit using [?] function. The Nursing Units that are displayed in the lookup are filtered based on the locations associated with the Operator Station of the logged in user.

    2. Admission Date From-To – Enter the Admission Date range in date format of dd/mm/yyyy or select the date range from Calendar function.

    3. Specialty – Enter the Specialty in alphanumeric format of 15 characters maximum length or select the Specialty using [?] function.

    4. Practitioner – Enter the name of the Practitioner in alphanumeric format of 15 characters maximum length or select the practitioner using [?] function.

    5. Encounter ID – Enter the Encounter ID in numeric format of 12 digits maximum length.

    6. Patient ID - Enter the Patient ID in alphanumeric format of maximum length as specified in MP Parameter or select the Patient ID using [?] function.

  2. On searching, Inpatient Details are displayed based on the entered criteria.

  3. Click ‘+’ hyperlink to view the Current Encounter Details.

  4. Click the ‘Encounter ID’ hyperlink to display the Prepare Discharge Advice screen. The screen displays Encounter Details and Nursing Unit Details.

  5. Enter the details in the mandatory (*) fields of Discharge Details tab and Other Details Tab.

Discharge Details Tab

Other Details Tab

  1. Click [Discharge Diagnosis] to view active problems of the patient and record the discharge diagnosis.

  2. Click [Certificate] to record medical certificate details.

    1. Certificate Type – Select the value as either ‘Medical’ or ‘Others’ from the list. On selecting ‘Medical’, Employment Details are displayed as recorded at the bottom of the screen. This field is mandatory.

    2. Medical Certificate No. – Enter the Medical Certificate Number in alphanumeric format of 20 characters maximum length. This field is mandatory.

    3. Leave From – The Admitted Date and Time of the patient is displayed.

    4. To – Enter or select the date and time as to when the patient is discharged from the facility. The current system date and time is displayed, by default. This field is mandatory.

    5. Number of Days – The total Number of Days of the leave period is displayed. This value is displayed by taking the difference of ‘Leave To’ and ‘Leave From’ date.

    6. Resume Activity – Select the check box to indicate that the patient is fit to resume activity. This field is displayed as selected, by default.

    7. Date – Enter or select the date as to when the patient can resume activity. This field is enabled only if the ‘Resume Activity’ field is selected and the date entered must be greater than the ‘Leave To’ date. The next consecutive date of the date mentioned in the ‘Leave To’ field is displayed, by default.

    8. Certificate Issued – Select the check box to indicate that certificate is issued to the discharged patient.

    9. Issued Date – Enter or select the date as to when the certificate was issued from the Calendar function. This field is enabled on selecting the ‘Certificate Issued’ field.

    10. Authorized By – Select the name of the Practitioner who authorized the certificate using the  [?] function. The treating practitioner is displayed by default. This field is mandatory.

    11. Remarks - Enter the Remarks in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length. This field is mandatory.

    Click [OK] to save the medical certificate, [Clear] to clear the entered data or [Cancel] to exit from the Certificate screen.

     

  3. Click [Record] to save the Discharge Advice prepared or [Clear] to clear the entered data. Click [Close] to exit from the screen.

  4. If the Discharge Type is selected as Deceased, on recording the details a message ‘Do you want to invoke Maintain Death Register’ is displayed. Click [OK] to record the first stage of Maintain Death Register details. You can finalize the details in the Maintain Death Register function. Click [Close] to close the Prepare Discharge Advice function. This message is displayed only if ‘Allow invoking Maintain Death Register in Prepare Discharge Advice’ check box is selected in the IP Parameter function. On recording the Prepare Discharge Advice, you can finalize the details of the death in the Maintain Death register function. The system creates a default record, if the user skips the process to invoke the Maintain Death Register function from Prepare Discharge Advice function. If the Maintain Death Register record is finalized, you cannot cancel the discharge advice prepared for a particular patient.

If prepared discharge advice is cancelled, the Death Register record will be cancelled.

  1. An Adaptive Menu is displayed with options to 'Register Referral' and 'Place Order' on successful completion of Preparing Discharge Advice. The adaptive menu is displayed if functions 'Place Order' and 'Register Inpatient Referral' are linked to the Quick Link Type 'Adaptive Menu' in 'Define Quick Link' function in Clinician Access module.

  2. Click Place Order to place order for the patient. The Orders screen gets displayed to capture the order details. Click Register Referral to refer the patient to another facility or Nursing Unit. The Register Referral Details Screen gets displayed to capture the referral details.

When you click the [Record] button, the system will check for the minimum data sets (MDS) for the encounter based on the configuration. If the MDS criteria are not met, the system will display the message “Criteria not met” with Check hyperlink. The [Record] Button will be disabled. You can click the Check hyperlink and see the MDS values which have not been met in the Minimum data set compliance dialog. Once you complete the required MDS, the [Record] Button will be enabled.
