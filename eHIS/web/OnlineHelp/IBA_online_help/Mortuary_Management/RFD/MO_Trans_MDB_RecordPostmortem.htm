Record Postmortem

This sub function is used to record the postmortem details of the deceased/body part which is in the postmortem area and has the postmortem status as 'Requested'.

Record Postmortem option is applicable to deceased/body parts in the postmortem area with 'Postmortem Required' field checked while registration and the postmortem status as Requested.  

 

This option is viewed under the following conditions.

Record Postmortem

  1. If postmortem has to be recorded for any deceased/body part with postmortem status as 'Requested' in any area, the deceased/body part has to be first assigned/reassigned to the Postmortem area and then Record Postmortem option is available for the deceased/body part.

  2. Click 'Deceased Name' hyperlink of the 'Deceased Patients' Tab to record postmortem with postmortem status as 'Requested'. Select the 'Reassign' Option from the popup window to reassign the deceased patient to the Postmortem Area and then click the Deceased Name hyperlink from the Postmortem Area.

  3. Record Postmortem screen currently displays one tab.

Registration Tab

  1. PM Requested By - By default the name of the practitioner who discharged/checked out the deceased patient is displayed as the practitioner who requested for postmortem. This is a mandatory field.

  2. Type of Postmortem - The type of postmortem is displayed as Medico Legal or Clinical as read only text.

  3. Purpose for performing postmortem - Enter the purpose for performing postmortem in alphanumeric format of 250 characters maximum length. This is a mandatory field.

Postmortem Performed By

  1. Practitioner Name - Enter the name of the practitioner who performed the postmortem in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length or select the name of the practitioner from the [?] function. The practitioners are defined in "Application Masters" module. This is a mandatory field.

  2. Designation - The designation of the practitioner is displayed and disabled as recorded in the "Application Masters" module.

  3. Role - Enter the role of the practitioner in alphanumeric format of 15 characters maximum length. This is a mandatory field.

Present During Postmortem

  1. Practitioner Name - Enter the name of the practitioner who was present during postmortem in alphanumeric format of  30 characters maximum length or select the practitioner using [?] function. The practitioners are defined in the "Application Masters" module. The practitioner selected as the practitioner who performed postmortem cannot be selected as the practitioner who was present during postmortem.

  2. Designation - The designation of the practitioner is displayed and disabled as recorded in the "Application Masters" module.

  3. Role - Enter the role of the practitioner in alphanumeric format of 15 characters maximum length.

Body Identified By

  1. Name - Enter the name of the person who identifies the details of body in alphanumeric format 30 characters maximum length.

  2. Remarks - Enter any remarks if required in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length.

Postmortem Status

  1. Patient Checked In At - The date and time when the deceased patient was registered to the mortuary is displayed as read only text.

  2. PM Started At - Enter the date and time as to when the postmortem started in dd/mm/yyyy hh:mi format or select the date using the calendar function. This is a mandatory field.

 

Scenario 1: Recording Postmortem for a Deceased Patient.

On entering the 'PM Started At' time, three more tabs appear in the screen to record the postmortem details as shown in the screen below.

The new tabs that appear are

Findings

Specimen

Tissue Procurement

  1. Wait Time Duration - The time difference between the 'PM Started At' time and 'Patient Checked in At' time is displayed as the wait time duration as read only text.

  2. PM Completed At - Enter the date and time of the completion time of the postmortem in dd/mm/yyyy hh:mi format or select the date using the calendar function. The entered time cannot be less than the 'PM Started At' time. On entering the PM Completion time, certain fields in other tabs should mandatorily be entered to record postmortem.

  3. Procedure Duration - On entering the 'PM Completed At' field, the time difference between the 'PM Completed At' time and 'PM Started At' time is automatically calculated and displayed as the procedure duration time as read only text.

  4. At any point in time, click [Record] to record the postmortem details even before recording the 'PM Completion At' time. Click [Cancel] to close the screen and the details entered are not recorded. The Postmortem Status of the deceased patient is displayed as 'In Progress'.

  5. Click the 'Deceased Name' hyperlink and select the [Record Postmortem] option to record the completion of postmortem.

'Registration' Tab

The details entered in the Registration tab are displayed. The displayed fields can be modified except the disabled fields.

Findings Tab

  1. This tab is used to record the details of findings that caused death.

  2. Postmortem Certified By - Enter the practitioner in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length or select the practitioner who certified the postmortem using [?] function. This field becomes mandatory on entering the 'PM Completed At' time.

Cause of Death

Disease directly leading to death  

  1. ICD Code – Select the ICD Code using the function [?]. The 'ICD Code Search' window from the Terminology and Coding Management module gets displayed.

  2. Cause – Enter the Cause of the death in the text box provided. You can enter a maximum of 200 characters.

Antecedent Cause

  1. Cause – Enter the Cause of the death in the text box provided. You can enter a maximum of 200 characters.

  2. Onset date - Enter or select the date of onset of the antecedent cause of death from the calendar function. This field becomes mandatory if the antecedent cause is selected.

Other Significant Condition  

  1. ICD Code – Select the ICD Code using the function [?]. The 'ICD Code Search' window from the Terminology and Coding Management module gets displayed.

  2. Condition – Enter the other significant Conditions that caused death in the text box provided. You can enter a maximum of 200 characters.

  3. The same details cannot be entered for Disease directly leading to death, Antecedent Cause and Other Significant Condition from the ICD Code Search Screen of the Terminology and Coding Management module.

  4. Manner of Death - Select the manner of death from the list. By default the value recorded at the time of registering the deceased to the mortuary is displayed. The listed values are defined in the "Manner of Death" function. This field becomes mandatory on entering the 'PM Completed At' time.

  5. Summary of Findings - Enter the summary of findings during postmortem in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length. This field becomes mandatory on entering the 'PM Completed At' time.

  6. Click [Record] to record the details.

Specimen Tab

  1. This tab is used to record the details of the specimens collected during postmortem.

Details of specimens collected:

  1. Specimen Description - Enter the description of the specimen in alphanumeric format of 40 characters maximum length.

  2. Specimen Type - Enter the type of the specimen in alphanumeric format of 50 characters maximum length. This field becomes mandatory on entering the Specimen Description.

  3. Anatomical Site - Select the anatomical site from the [?] function. The values displayed in the [?] function are defined in the "Application Masters" module. This field becomes mandatory on selecting the Specimen Description.

  4. The specimen type and anatomical site should have different values.

  5. The values populated in the screen are defined in the "Master Patient Index" module. Click the description hyperlink to select the value.

  6. Collection Time - Enter the time when the specimen was collected from the calendar function. This field becomes mandatory on selecting the Specimen Description.

  7. Specimen Collected By - Select the practitioner who collected the specimen using [?] function. This field becomes mandatory on selecting the Specimen Description.

  8. Specimen Handed Over to - Enter the name of the person to whom the specimen was handed over to in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length. This field becomes mandatory on selecting the Specimen Description.

  9. Specimen Handed Date/Time - Enter or select the date and time when the specimen was handed over from the calendar function. This field becomes mandatory on selecting the Specimen Description.

  10. Place Order – Click [Place Order], the Orders screen gets displayed. You can record consent for the order to be placed.

  11. Click [Record] to record the specimen details.

Tissue Procurement Tab

  1. This tab is used to record the tissue procurement details of the organs listed for donation while registering the deceased patient in the mortuary.

  2. Organs listed for Procurement - The organs selected for donation while registering the patient is listed. Select the required organ for procurement by checking the box.

  3. Tissue Procured - Select the required organ for procurement by checking the box.

  4. Procurement Time - Enter or select the procurement time from the calendar function. This field becomes mandatory on selecting the 'Tissue Procured' checkbox.

  5. Performed By - Enter the practitioner in alphanumeric format of 30 characters maximum length or select the name of the practitioner who performed the procurement of tissue from the [?] function. This field becomes mandatory on selecting the 'Tissue Procured' checkbox.

  6. Performed Date - Enter or select the date and time as to when tissue procurement was performed from the calendar function.

  7. Click [Record] to record the details.

 

Scenario 2: Recording Postmortem for a Body Part.

On entering the 'PM Started At' time for a Body Part, 2 more tabs appear as shown in the screen.

The new tabs are

Findings

Specimen

The details of the tabs are similar to the tabs while selecting to record postmortem for a deceased patient.

The 'Manner of Death' field is disabled in the 'Findings' Tab while recording postmortem for a body part.

On entering the 'PM Completed At' field in the Registration tab, the Procedure Duration is displayed.  After the mandatory fields are entered in all the other tabs, click [Record], the control shifts back to the Manage Deceased/Body Part main screen.

The postmortem status of the deceased patient is displayed as 'Completed' indicating that recording of postmortem details is complete.

After recording postmortem, the deceased patient/body part can be reassigned to the Release Area in order to release the deceased patient/body part from the mortuary by clicking the 'Deceased Name' hyperlink of the 'Deceased Patients' Tab and 'Patient Name' hyperlink of the 'Body Parts' Tab with postmortem status as 'Completed' and selecting the Reassign option.

Once the postmortem details are recorded for deceased patient/body part under clinical/medico legal postmortem and the postmortem status is displayed as 'Complete', the burial permit details can be entered for the deceased body.

The burial permit should mandatorily be generated in order to release the deceased patient/body part from the mortuary.

To generate the burial permit for a Deceased Patient, go to "Change Deceased Details" function, enter the query criteria and click [Search].

Select the 'Deceased Name' hyperlink and click the Burial Permit tab.

Enter the burial permit details as explained in the "Burial Permit" function.

If the Burial Permit details were already entered in the "Burial Permit" function before registering the patient to the mortuary, the burial permit details are displayed on selecting the "Burial Permit" tab which can be modified except the disabled fields.

To generate the burial permit for a Body Part, go to "Body Part Registration" function and select [Query] to query for the required body part. Enter the required query criteria and click [Search].

Select the 'Patient Name' hyperlink and select the Burial Permit tab which is displayed once postmortem has been completed for the body part.

Enter the burial permit details as explained in the "Burial Permit" function.

If the Burial Permit details were already entered in the "Burial Permit" function before registering the patient to the mortuary, the burial permit details are displayed on selecting the "Burial Permit" tab which can be modified except the disabled fields.
