1. The screen displays Order Date and Time, Order Category, Order Type, Patient details, Order ID,  Ordering Location, Encounter ID, Result Status, Charge Dtls View/Edit, Allergies, Active Problems, Event, Result, Unit, Low Value, High Value, Event and Status, Comments and Reporting Practitioner.

    1. Click Active Problems to display Problem List screen. The diagnosis recorded for the patient can be viewed. ‘Active Only’ radio button is selected displaying all the active diagnosis recorded for the patient, by default. Click ‘All’ radio button to view all the diagnosis recorded for the patient including diagnosis that are not active or those that are closed. Enter the query criteria and click [Search] to display a specific Problem List. Click [Clear] to clear the displayed data. Click [Authorize] to enter the password and to view problem list by authorized practitioners. The Problem List screen displays Type, Onset Date, Description, Stage, Code, Scheme, Status, Close Date, Supporting Diag Code. Click the ‘Description’ hyperlink to display Onset details like Date, Practitioner, Facility, Location, Service, Recorded Date and Recorded/Closed By. The Close details, Assessment details and Remarks are displayed if recorded.

    2. Click the [X] on the top right corner of the screen to exit from the Problem List screen.

    1. Click the Allergies hyperlink to display the Allergy List screen. The allergies recorded for the patient can be viewed. ‘Active Only’ radio button is selected displaying all the active allergies recorded for the patient, by default. Click ‘All’ radio button to view all the allergies recorded for the patient including allergies that are not active or those that are closed. Click the ‘Allergen’ hyperlink to display the Onset details like Onset Date, Practitioner, Facility, Location, Service, Recorded Date and Remarks on the right hand side of the screen. If the Allergy recorded no longer prevails for the patient and is closed, the Close details like Closed Date, Practitioner, Facility, Location, Service and Recorded Date are also displayed on the right hand side of the screen.

    2. You can also view the High Risk Conditions recorded for the patient. Click [Authorized] to enter the password and view high risk conditions recorded for the patient. The ‘View High Risk Consulting Patient’ screen displays to enter the password. Enter the password and click [OK] to confirm. [Normal Mode] is displayed in place of [Authorized]. ‘Active Only’ radio button is selected displaying all the active high risk conditions recorded for the patient, by default. Click ‘All’ radio button to view all the high risk conditions recorded for the patient including conditions that are not active or those that are closed. Click the ‘Description’ hyperlink to display the Onset details like Onset Date, Practitioner, Facility, Location, Service, Recorded Date and Remarks on the right hand side of the screen. If the high risk condition recorded no longer prevails for the patient and is closed, the Close details like Closed Date, Practitioner, Facility, Location, Service and Recorded Date are also displayed on the right hand side of the screen.

    3. Click the [X] on the top right corner of the screen to exit from the Allergy List screen.

  2. Select the Result hyperlink to Amend details of the Result. Click [Preview] to preview the result. Click [OK] to save the result or [Close] to exit from the screen.

  3. The name of the Reporting Practitioner is displayed. Select another Practitioner using [?] function, if required.

  4. Select the Status as Not Applicable, Normal or Abnormal from the list for the Event.

  5. Click Comments hyperlink. Enter the Comments, if any.

  6. Click ‘Press Link to Authorize’ hyperlink to Authorize the Result. A message is displayed. Click [OK] to Authorize the Result.   

  7. ‘Press Link to Authorize’ hyperlink is displayed as ‘Authorized’.

  8. Click [Record] to confirm authorizing the Report. Click [Cancel] to get back to the main screen without authorizing the Report.
