AE Checkout

From this function you can Close an active encounter of an emergency patient and Record the outcome details of the encounter.

Check out is a process of concluding an encounter against a clinic at the end of consultation. This function is used when the patient’s stay is complete and patient is either being sent home after treatment or is being referred to another facility for further treatment. AE Checkout function is used to close an active encounter and also allows concluding an active episode, issuing a medical certificate, putting the patient in wait list or referring the patient to another provider for further treatment.

 

Hospital and Professional Charges are displayed on recording the financial details of a patient. The patient payable amount towards Hospital charge as well as Doctor’s charge is displayed. Professional Charge is displayed along with the Hospital Charge, only if the billing details and visit registration rules are defined for the Clinic. The doctor’s charge has to be a part of the panel, if the Professional charge has to be set as zero. If the Professional Charge is set as a Service, you cannot set the doctor’s fee as zero. You can instead associate the Hospital Charges to make up the Professional fees, when the doctor does not charge the patient for the services.

The system does not allow AE Check Out if orders are pending when the ‘Do not allow AE checkout’ option if Orders are Pending is selected in AE Parameter.

 

Process Flow Diagram

Checking Out Emergency Patients

From the AE Transactions, select the AE Checkout Function.

AE Checkout screen gets displayed.

  1. By default, the patients who have their attendance registered are displayed in the screen. Enter the query criteria to select any required patient. The displayed patient records are segregated based on the Location/Practitioner/Room combination. The patient listing is sorted based on the priority zone under the following sub tabs, namely, Critical, Semi-Critical, Non-Critical, Unassigned and All. <Related Field Description>

    1. Practitioner - The name of the Practitioner associated to the patient gets displayed. Practitioner name field is left blank, if the Practitioner is not associated to the selected patient.

    2. Priority – The Priority Zone assigned to the patient gets displayed. The colors used to represent the zones are:

    3. Red – Critical Zone

    4. Yellow– Semi Critical Zone

    5. Green – Non Critical Zone

    1. Date/Time In – The Date and Time the patient checked in to the facility gets displayed.

    2. Bed/Bay No – The Bed/Bay number assigned to the patient gets displayed.

    3. Patient Name – The Patient Name gets displayed.

    4. Patient ID – The Patient ID gets displayed.

    5. Wait Time – The Wait Time for the patient in the facility gets displayed.

    6. Status – The Status of the patient gets displayed. The various statuses applicable to a patient queue are ‘Checked In’, ‘Assigned Treatment Area’, ‘Undergoing Treatment’, ‘Checked Out’ and ‘Consultation in Progress’.

    7. Current Location – The Location Type/Location against which the patient visit is registered gets displayed.

    8. Disaster – If the patient is a victim of Disaster, ‘yes’ is displayed or ‘no’ is displayed in the field.

    9. Case of Trauma - If case patient is registered under case of Trauma, ‘yes’ is displayed or ‘no’ is displayed in the field.

  2. Click [Refresh] to refresh the Patient records or re-query the screen manually. By default, this screen is refreshed automatically based on the Refresh value parameter setup.

  3. Click Patient Details hyperlink of the required patient to Checkout the patient.

  4. Enter the details in the Checkout screen to perform the Checkout. <Related Field Description>

  5. Click [Certificate] in the Check Out screen to issue a medical certificate. Click [OK] to save the certificate, [Clear] to clear the details entered or [Cancel] to exit from the screen.

  6. Click [Create Waitlist] to create a wait list for the patient if schedule is not available. This button is enabled if the ‘Type’ in the Check Out screen is selected as a value with the ‘Use at Visit Conclusion’ check box selected in the Disposition Type function of the Application Masters module.

  7. Click [Conclude Episode] to conclude episode.

    If the Episode of the selected visit has to be concluded, click [Conclude Episode]. Episode cannot be concluded for patients who are asked to come back on a future date, in such cases the button is disabled. Conclude Episode screen displays the Service Start and End date of the current episode. You can enter the Notes pertaining to the Episode.

  8. Click [Record] to record the Checkout details or click [Cancel] to return to patient queue screen.  

  9. You cannot checkout the patient, if outstanding patient bills exist for the encounter. An error message 'Outstanding Patient Bill(s) exists for this encounter. Cannot Proceed' is displayed. Click [OK]. A hyperlink 'Outstanding Bills Settlement' is displayed. Click the hyperlink to settle bill details. The Bill Number and outstanding amount are displayed with the Bill Number displayed as a hyperlink. Click the hyperlink to view and settle bill details. A Receipt Number is generated on clicking [OK].

  10. The Visit Charge Details screen is displayed with the charge details. Click [Continue] to proceed.

  11. You cannot checkout the patient without settling the bill details, if the check box 'Allow AE checkout with bill settlement' is selected in 'AE Parameter' function. An authorized user can issue discounts and settle the bill amount of a patient. A visit bill number is generated and the bill settlement details screen pops-up on visit bill generation. Enter the payment details to generate bill-cum-receipt for the respective patient encounter. A receipt document type and number is generated.

  12. The system prompts a pop-up message if patient requires a referral registration. Click [OK] in the message dialog to register a referral and the Register Referral Details screen appears. Click [Cancel] to return to patient queue screen.

  13. Enter the details in the mandatory fields in Register Referral Details screen. Click [Clear] to clear the entered details if required.

  14. On entering the details, click [Record] to add the patient as referral and Referral Details are displayed. Click [Clear] to clear the displayed details. Click [Refresh] to refresh the screen and on completion of registration of referrals, click [Close] to return to the patient queue screen.

    The following details are displayed:

    1. Referral ID - The Referral Identification Number generated for referral is displayed and hyperlinked. You can view the referral details on clicking hyperlink.

    2. Preferred On – The preferred date of referral is displayed.

    3. Referred To – The destination facility of referral is displayed.

    4. Specialty – The Specialty name to which referral has been recorded is displayed.

    5. Location - The location to which referral has been recorded is displayed.

    6. Practitioner - The name of the practitioner against whom referral has been recorded is displayed.

    7. Appt. No – If appointment has been confirmed using the referral identification number, the Appointment Number is displayed. You can proceed with booking an appointment using the [Book Appt] hyperlink.

 

  1. ‘Recall Details’ is disabled for emergency patient check out.

  2. This function can also be invoked from ‘Current Emergency Patients’, or ‘Current Emergency Patients using Bed/Bay chart’. This direct option is basically provided for users who do not have access to ‘Current Emergency Patients’ and ‘Current Emergency Patients using Bed/Bay chart’ function to record the Checkout details.

  3. When you click the [Record] button, the system will check for the minimum data sets (MDS) for the encounter based on the configuration. If the MDS criteria are not met, the system will display the message “Criteria not met” with Check hyperlink. The [Record] Button will be disabled. You can click the Check hyperlink and see the MDS values which have not been met in the Minimum data set compliance dialog. Once you complete the required MDS, the [Record] Button will be enabled.
