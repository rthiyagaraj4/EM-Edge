Transfusion of Blood Units

From this function you can Transfuse the blood units and Print the blood unit reports. You can Query to view the details.

The Transfusion of Blood Units function is used to transfuse the blood units issued for a patient. The blood units are issued in the ‘Allocate/Xmatch/Issue - Blood Units’ function. You can search for the issued blood units and transfuse the selected unit through this function. Option is available to print the unit reports of blood units. You can transfuse the blood units that are issued. The color codes displayed at the bottom of the screen denote the following.

– Units issued in emergency

– Autologous Unit

– Stat (to be issued immediately)

– Urgent

– Directed Unit

– Routine

– Multi allocation Unit

Transfusion of Blood Units

From the Transactions > BT Operations, double-click Transfusion of Blood Units function.

The Transfusion of Blood Units screen gets displayed.

  1. Click [List of Values] to select the Specimen Number to transfuse the blood unit.

  2. Click [Execute Query] to view the patient details, Product Category and Unit details. <Related Field Description>

  3. Select a record and click [Transfuse] to transfuse the selected blood unit.  On clicking [Transfuse], the system displays a message ‘This Unit Number and Product Code is Transfused to the patient. Please confirm to commit’ for confirmation. Click [OK] to record transfusion details, or [Cancel] to quit the process. The transfusion Start and End Time screen in displayed. <Related Field Description>

  4. Note: If the Transfused by Practitioner ID is not selected as the logged in user name, an error ‘Error in Practitioner Selection’ is displayed.

  5. Click [Save & Close] to transfuse the blood unit to the patient or [Cancel] to quit the transfusion process.  After the transfusion of blood unit, the status of the blood unit is changed from Issued to Transfused.

  6. Click [Print] to print unit report. You can print unit reports of Allocated, Compatible/Suitable, Issued, Transfused units or All the Units together. A report is generated with the blood units, product/categories and status along with the reserved date.

  7. Click [Details] to view the patient details.

  8. Click [Antibodies] to view the antibodies recorded for the patient. The [Antibodies] gets enabled if antibodies are recorded for the selected patient.

  9. Click [Transfusion History] to view the previous transfusion details of the patient, if any.

  10. Click [Requirements] to view the transfusion requirements recorded for the patient, if any.

  11. Click [View Xmatch Results] to invoke Cross Match Tests screen to view the cross match results. Click [OK] to close the Cross Match Tests screen.

  12. Click [View Multi-Allocation] to view the units allocated for multiple specimens. Click [Close] to close the screen.

  13. Click [Product/Category Unit Details] to view category and product details of the unit. Click [Close] to close the screen.

  14. Click [View Incompatible Returns] to view the details of the blood units that are returned due to incompatibility.

[View Incompatible Returns], [View Multi Allocation], and [View X Match Results] get enabled if they are applicable for the selected blood unit.
