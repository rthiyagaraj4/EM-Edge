Enter the following details:

 

  1. Procedure Set – Select the required procedure set from the list. The listed values are displayed based on Procedures created in the MR module. This field is mandatory.

  2. Procedure Code - Enter the Procedure Code in alphanumeric format of 10 characters or select it by clicking [Search]. This field is mandatory. On selecting the Procedure Code, [!] is displayed, which shows the long description of the procedure on clicking it. Click [OK] to close the long description screen of the procedure.

  3. Description – On selection of the Procedure Code, the description of the code is displayed and cannot be modified. This field is mandatory.

  4. Performed At – Select a value from the list indicating where the procedure was performed.

  5. Priority – Select the Priority from the list.

  6. Starting Time – This field indicates the start date/time of the procedure. The system date/time is displayed by default, which can be changed. Enter the date/time in date format of dd/mm/yyyy hh:mm or select the date/time from the Calendar function. The date/time can be backdated, but it should not be lesser than the encounter start date/time and should not be greater than the system date/time. This field is mandatory.

  7. Duration – Enter the duration taken to perform the procedure. The entry is made in terms of hours and minutes that is entered in the two fields provided next to the duration field. The values are entered in numeric format.

  8. Ending Time – This field is used to indicate the end time of the procedure. The end time is calculated based on the value entered in the duration field. If the duration is not specified, the end time can be entered in date format of dd/mm/yyyy hh:mm or select the date/time from the Calendar function. The date/time can be backdated, but it must not be lesser than the start date/time of the procedure and must not be greater than the system date/time.

  9. Location - Select the required Location using the [?] function. The Location listed are created in the Application Masters module 'Care Location Type' function with indicators OT (All), OT (Specific) and Procedure Unit. Select Procedure Unit Locations to identify the procedure as a Non OT procedure. Select OT Locations to identify the procedure as OT procedures.

  10. Practitioner - Select the practitioner who performed the Procedure from [?] function. The displayed list is limited to the practitioners associated to the logged in Facility. The practitioner must also be associated to the specialty that the patient is admitted/registered into to be listed in this field. There are two fields for recording the practitioners involved in the procedure. Recording one practitioner is mandatory.

  11. Anesthetists - Select the anesthetist involved in the Procedure from the list. The displayed list is limited to the practitioners with Practitioner Type set as Anesthetist and those that are associated to the logged in Facility. There are three fields for recording the Anesthetists involved in the procedure.

  12. Scrub Nurse - Select the scrub nurse involved in the Procedure from the list. The displayed list is limited to the Practitioners with Practitioner Type set as Nurse and those that are associated to the logged in Facility.

  13. Anesthesia – Select the Anesthesia used to perform the procedure. The values listed are set in the master function ‘Anesthesia’ in the Applications Masters module.

  14. Anesthesia Details - Enter the anesthesia details in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length or choose the text required by invoking the ‘Canned Text’ option which is displayed as a button at the bottom portion of the screen.

  15. Indication - Enter the Indication details in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length or choose the text required by invoking the ‘Canned Text’ option, which appears as a button at the bottom portion of the screen.

  16. Monitoring - Enter the details recorded while monitoring the patient in alphanumeric length or choose the text required by invoking Canned Text’ option, which appears as a button at the bottom portion of the ‘Procedure Recording’ screen.

  17. HPI – Enter the HPI (History of Present Illness) in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length or choose the text required by invoking the ‘Canned Text’ option, which appears as a button at the bottom portion of the ‘Procedure Recording’ screen.

  18. Procedure Summary - Enter the procedure summary in alphanumeric format of 2000 characters maximum length or choose the text required by invoking the ‘Canned Text’ option, which appears as a button at the bottom portion of the screen.

  19. Findings - Enter the findings of the procedure in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length or choose the text required by invoking the ‘Canned Text’ option, which appears as a button at the bottom portion of the screen.

  20. Impression - Enter the impression in alphanumeric format of 200 characters maximum length or choose the text required by invoking the ‘Canned Text’ option, which appears as a button at the bottom portion of the screen.

  21. Disposition - Enter the disposition details in alphanumeric format of 200 characters maximum length or choose the text required by invoking the ‘Canned Text’ option, which appears as a button at the bottom portion of the screen.

  22. Events - Enter the events details in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length or choose the text required by invoking the ‘Canned Text’ option, which appears as a button at the bottom portion of the screen.

  23. Post Procedure Std. Orders - Enter the standard orders that may be placed after the completion of the procedure in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length or choose the text required by invoking the ‘Canned Text’ option, which appears as a button at the bottom portion of the screen.

  24. Specimen Sent - Enter the details of specimen sent in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length or choose the text required by invoking the ‘Canned Text’ option, which appears as a button at the bottom portion of the screen.
