Ectopic Pregnancy

Ectopic means "out of place". In an ectopic pregnancy, a fertilized egg implants outside the uterus. The egg settles in the fallopian tubes more than 95% in most cases. This is why ectopic pregnancies are commonly called "tubal pregnancies." The egg can also implant in the ovary, abdomen, or the cervix, also referred to as cervical or abdominal pregnancies. None of these areas has as much space or nurturing tissue as a uterus for a pregnancy to develop. As the fetus grows, it will eventually burst the organ that contains it. This can cause severe bleeding and endanger the mother's life. A classical ectopic pregnancy never develops into a live birth.
