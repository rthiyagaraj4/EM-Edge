Patient Social History

From this function you can Record and View Patient Social History that reflect the clinical condition of a patient.

Patient Social History is a statement about a patient with clinical relevance and forms a part of the patient record. Social History are referred to as point of care documentation usually recorded once when the patient comes for the facility for the first time, and all such recording form an important and integral part of the patient history. The user can set up privileges for the practitioners that govern the access rights of practitioners to record or view a social history of a patient. The system facilitates maintaining a draft copy of the history until it is signed or authorized by the practitioner recording the history or by another practitioner based on their access rights. A history once recorded can be modified based on the setting for the note type. A note can be sent for review before making it a part of the patient history. Analysis can be performed based on some components of the note.

Recording Patient Social History

From the Clinician Access > Desktop Menu > select required patient > Patient Chart Menu > Documentation, select Patient Social History function.

The Patient Social History screen gets displayed.
