Discharged but not billed

From this function you can Query on the inpatients discharged from a specific ward in the facility and not billed for the services utilized.  

The Discharged but not billed function is used to query the inpatient details who have been discharged from the facility, but owe amount for the services utilized. The details of the inpatient’s, who are not charged for the services, but discharged from the facility get listed. You have an option to search for the inpatient records in a specific ward or based on the discharge date. The patient ID, encounter number, admission date, discharge date, room,  bed class, bed type, practitioner assigned and the financial details recorded for the respective patient gets displayed.  

Querying Inpatients who are discharged, but not billed for the services:

From the Queries > Discharge Bills, double-click Discharged but not billed function.

The Discharged but not billed screen gets displayed.

  1. Click Calendar function to select a date range and to view the inpatient’s, who are yet to be discharged from a particular ward in the facility. The details of the inpatients who has been discharged during the specified date range and owing amount for the services utilized, gets displayed.  

  2. The Ward Codes get displayed. The Nursing Units defined in the Inpatient and Day Care Management module are listed. Click on the specific Ward and the inpatients who have been discharged during the specified date range are listed. Click [Clear Record] to clear the record from the list.

  3. Click [Backward Arrow] to navigate to the previous ward code. Click [Forward Arrow] to navigate to the subsequent ward code. Based on the ward selected, the patient details are displayed.

  4. Click each ward to view the list of inpatients discharged from the respective ward. Click [Clear Record] to clear the selected inpatient’s details from the list. The patient’s name, gender and the age gets displayed in the fields at the bottom of the screen. The details change on clicking each individual patient record.
