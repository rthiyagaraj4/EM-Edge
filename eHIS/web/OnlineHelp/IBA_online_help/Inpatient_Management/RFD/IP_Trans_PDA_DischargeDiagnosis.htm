Record Discharge Diagnosis

On invoking the function, the Diagnosis Profile of the patient is displayed, which includes Diagnosis Description, Accuracy, Encounter Stage, onset date of the Diagnosis, Status and Detail. This section is empty if the patient has no diagnosis (problems) recorded in the facility or enterprise. The Diagnoses displayed are only the active diagnosis for the patient that may have been recorded across encounters. The records displayed are grouped by the diagnosis set.

  1. Enter the details in the mandatory (*) fields. <Related Field Description>

  2. Click [Collapse] to minimize the Update Diagnosis Profile frame or [Expand] to expand the Update Diagnosis Profile frame.

  3. Click [Authorize Mode] to record sensitive diagnosis details.

    In the event of a sensitive diagnosis being selected for a patient, the system asks for Authorization. This Authorization is displayed only if the logged in practitioner has the privilege of recording a Sensitive Diagnosis as defined in the ‘User Access Rights’ master function in Medical Records module. If the privilege is not given to the logged in user, the system displays an error message and restricts the recording of the sensitive diagnosis for the patient. The Authorization is validated by means of providing a password. If the password specified is wrong, the system stops you from continuing with the recording. Once the correct password is provided, the Description of the Sensitive Diagnosis is defaulted onto the corresponding field and you can record this diagnosis for the patient.

    On successful completion of the recording, the record is moved to the ‘Current Diagnosis Profile’ section. The sensitive diagnosis is viewed by clicking on the [Authorize Mode] and providing the correct password. In such an event, the sensitive diagnosis is displayed under the ‘Current Problem’ section. On Click of the [Cancel], the reason entered is ignored and the process is aborted.

    Enter the password as specified in the function ‘User Access Rights’ to edit or enter the sensitive. After entering the password click [OK] to confirm or click [Cancel] to cancel the current job and the control shifts back to the Record Diagnosis screen with the [Authorize mode] changed to [Normal Mode].

  4. Click [Record] to record the diagnosis, [Clear] to clear the entered data or [Cancel] to exit from the Record Diagnosis screen.

  5. On recording the diagnosis, the details are displayed on the top half of the screen under Current Diagnosis Profile.

  6. Click Diagnosis Description hyperlink to modify the recorded details.

    The status of a diagnosis recorded for a patient can be changed based on the periodic assessment of the patient. If the identified diagnosis has been resolved for the Patient, then that problem can be closed. To change the status of a diagnosis select the problem record displayed in the ‘Current Diagnosis Profile’ section by clicking on the code or description hyperlink.

    Mark Error

    A Diagnosis recorded can be marked as an erroneous record if it was recorded by mistake. To mark a Diagnosis record as ‘In Error’ choose the Diagnosis from the ‘Current Diagnosis Profile’ section. Select the status as ‘In Error’ and records the ‘As on’ date.

    On selecting the status as ‘In Error’, the Remarks field becomes mandatory. Enter the Remarks. Click [Record] to save the changes.

  7. Click [Record] to save the updated record.
