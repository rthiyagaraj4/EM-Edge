Treatment

You can record a treatment for an arch, quadrant, a tooth or a tooth range. Treatment can be recorded using the Treatment tab in the dental chart. The following figure shows the Baseline Chart displayed on clicking the Treatment tab. The Treatment tab is used to schedule a treatment and to perform it on the scheduled date. You can choose to record a treatment by clicking on the image or selecting a treatment from the data entry area. To select a treatment by clicking on the image, select the check box adjacent to the tooth number and select the treatment. On selecting the treatment by clicking on the image, the details are populated automatically in the respective fields and the field is disabled. You can record the remarks and select the Treatment from the list.

 

  1. You can record the Treatment using the Data Entry Area or the Dental Chart.

  2. On entering the treatment details and clicking on [Record], the Order Entry screen gets displayed with the Orderable, Order Type, Priority, Quantity, Frequency and the Duration. You can update the Start Date, priority and the charges. If the patient has a open encounter with the facility, the charges for the treatment are calculated in the respective encounter. If any old encounters exist with the task for a patient, the old encounter is cancelled and a new treatment is created with the new encounter, if there are multiple tasks associated to a treatment.

  3. Click [Record] to record the details or [Reset] to clear the details.

  4. An Order ID is generated.

  5. A system-defined image gets displayed on the crown, root or on the tooth once the treatment is recorded.  

  6. A system-defined image gets displayed below the surface of the tooth. Click on the image to view the treatment details.

  7. The treatments recorded are listed under the Treatments To Do menu on the right-hand side of the screen.

  8. The Treatments that are completed are listed under the Treatments Done menu.

    The treatments, which are complete, are listed under the Treatments Done menu. You can mark the outcome of the treatment or link treatment details to task to the treatment. Click adjacent to the Treatment Name to view the tasks associated to the Treatment. You can associate Treatment Details to a task, view the treatment associated details and remarks, and link Diagnosis or Condition to the treatment. You can record Composition Details, Complication Details and Progress Notes for individual task, or the treatment, if it is complete.

    Click the hyperlink to record Progress Notes. Progress Notes is enabled when a task is registered or at the treatment level when individual tasks associated to the treatment are registered. The notes differ based on the order. You can edit the notes until it is signed-off. You can click the history area to edit or view the notes that are recorded for a tooth. The treatment name is displayed as a hyperlink. Click the treatment name to view the tasks that are associated to the treatment.

    You can record the complications when the treatment is being done.

    Click on the hyperlink to view the Complications associated to the respective Task. You can record the details until the task is marked as data entry completed. The first task that is associated to the complication is highlighted in bold. When the task is marked as completed, you can only view the details and cannot make any changes.

    Complication Details Entry

    1. Complication – Select a Complication from the list. The Complications  defined in the OH Code Setup menu gets listed. An additional value 'Others (Free Text)' is available in the list apart from the values defined through 'Complication' function. Select 'Others (Free Text)' to record additional details on any complication. You can enter a complication in alpha numeric format with a maximum of 100 characters when the value is selected as 'Others (Free Text)'.

    2. [Record] - Click [Record] to record the complications. On clicking [Record], the details get listed in the screen under Complication Details header.

    3. [Clear] - Click [Clear] to clear the complication that is selected.

    4. Select – Select the check box to select and delete the respective Complication. A confirmation message 'Tasks Data Entry will be completed. Do you want to proceed?' is displayed on selecting the check box. Click [OK] to proceed or [Cancel] to abort the process.

    5. [Delete] – Click [Delete] to delete associated the complication.

    6. [Close] - Click [Close] to exit from the screen.

    7. Data Entry Completed – Select the check box, if association of Complication to the task is complete.

    8. Task - The task name is displayed as a hyperlink. The composition details associated to the task are displayed on selecting the task.

    You can record the components while planning the treatment or completing the treatment. Click Composition Details hyperlink to record the details.

    Composition Details Entry

    1. Composition Class – Select a Composition Class from the list. The Composition Class defined in the OH Code Setup menu gets listed.

    2. Composition - Select a Composition from the list. The Compositions associated to the Composition Class using the function Composition in the OH Code Setup menu are listed.

    3. [Record] - Click [Record] to record the compositions. On clicking [Record], the composition details get listed in the screen.

    4. Select – Select the check box to select and to delete the associated composition details.

    5. [Delete] – Click [Delete] to delete associated the components.

    6. [Clear] - Click [Clear] to clear the entered data.

    7. [Close] - Click [Close] to exit from the screen.

    8. Data Entry Completed – Select the check box, if association of composition class to the task is complete.

    9. Task - The task name is displayed as a hyperlink. The composition details are displayed on selecting the task.

    Click Remarks to view the remarks recorded by the dentist while registering the treatment for the patient. Click [Close] to exit from the screen.

    Click ‘View Treatment Details’ hyperlink to view the associated treatment details. The treatment details associated to the task are listed on the upper part of the screen. The total charge of treatment, patient charge and paid amount are listed for each task. The fields 'Included' and 'Approval Required' indicates if the service associated to the treatment includes policy level coverage and if it requires Preapproval. If the policy level coverage is available and preapproval required, the value is displayed as 'Yes'. If the policy level coverage is not available and preapproval not required, the value is displayed as 'No'. The data entry status is ‘incomplete’ if the check box ‘Data Entry Completed’ is cleared after associating the treatment details to the task. The status is set as ‘Completed’ on selecting the check box ‘Data Entry Completed’. Click [Close] to exit from the screen.

    Click on the Task name under the Treatment Name on the right-hand side menu. The data gets populated in the respective fields under the Baseline Chart.

    Click ‘Treatment Details’ hyperlink to associate or delete the Treatment details associated to the selected task.

    You can delete the treatment details associated to the task.

    1. Select the check box to select and delete the associated composition details.

    2. Click [Delete] to delete associated the components.

    1. Select a Composition Class from the list. The Composition Class defined in the OH Code Setup menu gets listed.

    2. Select a Composition from the list. The Compositions associated to the Composition Class using the function Composition in the OH Code Setup menu are listed.

    3. Click [Record] to record the compositions. On clicking [Record], the composition details get listed in the screen.

    4. Click [Clear] to reset the entries made or [Close] to exit from the screen.

    You cannot make duplicate entries, if the Composition Class and Composition is already associated to the task.

    The Composition Details Entry screen gets displayed. You can associate or delete the Treatment details associated to the selected task.

    Select the check box ‘Data Entry Completed’ to mark the treatment or task as complete. A confirmation message ‘Task treatment details entry is completed’ gets displayed. You cannot associate additional treatment details or delete the associated treatment details, if you select the check box ‘Data Entry Completed’. If the check box is not selected, the treatment details associated is shown as incomplete. The Data Entry Status is displayed as ‘Complete’, if the check box is selected.

    Select a value from the list. The system defined values are ‘Success’ and ‘Failure’. On selecting the ‘Outcome’ as ‘Success’, the ‘Status’ is updated as ‘Performed’.

    On registering a task with the  External Location Applicable as marked, option is enabled to record send out and acknowledgement details. Option is available to record the details when it is received. Additional tab called Result Entry is also displayed to view details and record Composition or Complication details for a task that is complete.. The Clinical Notes associated to the selected Task is invoked, when the send out option is selected enabling the user to record the details and print the recorded details. Select the check box 'Performed in OT', if the operation is performed in Operating Theater.

    This tab is enabled if the 'External Performing Location' check box is selected for the respective task.

    Enter the following details:

    Received Details

    1. Received  Date - The current date and time is displayed, by default. Click the Calendar function to back date until the registration date and time.

    2. Received Personnel Details - Enter the name of the person who has received the results.

    3. Received - Select the check box to indicate that the result is received or clear the check box if the result is not received.

    4. Remarks - Enter the remarks in alphanumeric format of 500 characters maximum length.

    5. Lab Price - Enter the lab price to indicate the price for processing the order and result reporting.

    6. Clinical Notes - The note type associated to the task is displayed. Click the hyperlink to record and print the details. Once recorded, the legend changes to View Clinical Notes Details through which you can view the recorded details.

    7. Click [Record] to record the details or [Reset] to reset the details.

    This tab is enabled only in the 'Send Out' details are recorded.

    Enter the following details:

    1. Received Date  - Click the Calendar function to select the date. The date is defaulted to the current system date and you can select a date until the sent out date.

    2. Receipt No/Invoice No - Enter the receipt number or the invoice number in alphanumeric format of nine characters maximum length.

    External Location Acknowledgement Details

    1. Received Personnel Detail - Enter the details of the person who has received the order.

    2. Expected delivery date - Click the Calendar function to select the expected delivery date.

    3. Acknowledged - Select the check box to acknowledge the order received.

    4. Clinical Notes - The note type associated to the task is displayed. Click the hyperlink to record and print the details. Once recorded, the legend changes to View Clinical Notes Details through which you can view the recorded details.

    5. Click [Record] to acknowledge or [Cancel] to cancel the process.

    This tab is enabled only if the 'Performing Location Applicable' check box is selected for the respective task.

    Enter the following details:

    Sent From Details

    1. Practitioner - Click [?] function to select a Practitioner. The Practitioners associated to the selected location are listed. The logged in Practitioner name is displayed, by default.

    Sent To Details

    1. Performing Location - Select a Performing Location from the list. The values recorded through the 'External Performing Location' are listed.

    2. Sent to Personnel - Enter the details in alphanumeric format of 100 characters maximum length.

    3. Sent Out Date - Click the Calendar function to select the date when the order is sent. The current date is displayed, by default. You can select the back-date until the registration date.

    4. Clinical Notes - The note type associated to the task is displayed. Click the hyperlink to record and print the details. Once recorded, the legend changes to View Clinical Notes Details through which you can view the recorded details.

    5. Click [Record] to record the details or [Reset] to reset the details.

  9. Click to view a summary of the Gingival Status and the Periodontal Status recorded for the respective patient. A Dentist can record the Gingival Status on the Sextant or the Quadrant, based on the value set for ‘Gingival Status Recording’ in the ‘Setup Parameters for Facility’ function. The values recorded against the Sextant or the Quadrant gets displayed. The Gingival Status Summary and Periodontal Summary options are displayed as hyperlinks. If there is no data recorded, then the options 'Gingival Status' and 'Periodontal Status' are displayed as normal text and clicking is disabled.

    The Periodontal Chart created for a patient using Periodontal Charting under the Charting menu in the Patient Chart gets listed. You can search and view the summary of a specific Evaluation and Baseline Chart. Click the Periodontal Summary hyperlink to view the Periodontal summary.

    Enter the following to view the Summary:

    1. Chart – Select a Chart to view the summary. The Baseline and the Evaluation Charts, which are complete, gets listed.

    2. [Search] – Click [Search] to view the summary of the selected Periodontal Chart.

    The number of conditions recorded indicates the number of tooth against which the gingival status is recorded. The Sum and the Average of the respective Gingival Status is recorded and computed by the system.

    The Gingival Status recorded for a patient using the Gingival Status tab under the Condition tab gets listed. The numbers assigned for each Gingival Status are displayed as a hyperlink. The tooth numbers for which the Gingival Status is recorded gets displayed below the respective Gingival Status number. The tooth number, condition type and the gingival status recorded against the tooth are displayed along with the value.

    To view the summary for a selected gingival status, click the respective gingival status number, which is displayed as a hyperlink. The number of conditions recorded indicates the number of tooth against which the gingival status is recorded. The Sum and the Average of the respective Gingival Status is recorded and computed by the system.

  10. Click to close the existing chart and add new Baseline Charts to record conditions and treatments for the patient. The Baseline Charts, which are closed and open, are displayed under the respective status. If the chart is for a patient in the deciduous age range, the chart is displayed with the status as [D].

  11. Click to view the Diagnosis details and the Clinical Notes recorded by the Practitioner for the patient. Click Dentist Comments hyperlink to record comments in alphanumeric format of 1000 characters maximum length. You can record comments only on a active chart.

    Click Clinical Notes hyperlink to record Clinical Notes for the selected patient.

    You can view the remarks and the treatment details associated to the task by clicking the respective hyperlink. Click 'Link Diagnosis/Condition' hyperlink to associate a condition or a diagnosis to the treatment. Select a Diagnosis from the list. The conditions linked to the diagnosis using the ‘Link Diagnosis’ hyperlink get listed. The diagnosis details recorded while linking the condition gets displayed. Select the check box to associate the diagnosis. Click [Record] to save the details. On clicking [Record], the details are displayed on the top of the screen. Click [Clear] to clear the details and select any other diagnosis or [Close] to exit from the screen. You can dissociate the linked diagnosis by selecting the check box for respective diagnosis and clicking [Delete]. You can delete the diagnosis associated to the treatment by selecting the check box.

  12. Click to print a dental chart with treatment details. The print option is valid for Closed Baseline Charts and Treatments. You can select a date and the numbering system based on which the dental chart has to be printed. A Dental Chart Print' screen is displayed with a preview of the tooth images along with the icons, conditions and treatments recorded. The comments recorded by a dentist in the ‘Treatment’ tab through the ‘Dentist Comments’ hyperlink is displayed. You can update the comments. Click [Print] to print the selected dental chart or [Close] to cancel the print process.

    The chart also displays the external treatments and treatments that are planned and performed. The name of the patient, age and gender are displayed. Details like the name of the user who has created the chart, validity period and the numbering system used to create the chart are displayed. The name of the user is displayed as the 'Signed Name' and the 'Signed Date' is defaulted to the system date. You can enter the Dentist Comments in an active chart before printing the dental chart. You have an option to select language and print chart in the required language.
