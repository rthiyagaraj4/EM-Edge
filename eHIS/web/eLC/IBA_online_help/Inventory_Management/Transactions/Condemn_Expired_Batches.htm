Condemn Batches

From this function you can condemn all the expiry and non-expiry items or batches i.e., items/batches with or without expiry dates.

Items marked for condemning will not be available for subsequent transactions based on configuration.  Approval from higher authorities may be required before condemning.

Condemning Batches

From the ST Transactions > Expiry Management, select the Condemned Batches function.

The Condemned Batches screen gets displayed.

  1. Click ‘Document’ hyperlink to invoke the Modify Document Details screen.

    Enter the following details to modify the required document details:

     

    1. Doc Type – Select the required Condemned Document Type from the list. The Document Type, as set in ‘ST Facility Parameter’ is displayed, by default. The documents with automatic number generation are displayed in the list. This is a mandatory field. You can click the Document hyperlink and update the details anytime until finalization by launching this popup.Doc No. The Document Number is system generated and you cannot enter value in the field.

    2. Doc Ref – Enter the Document Reference in alphanumeric format of 15 characters maximum length.

    3. Doc Date - The system date is displayed as the Document Date, by default. This is a mandatory field.

    4. Remarks – Enter the required remarks for condemning the expiry/non-expiry items. You can enter free-text of up-to 200 characters in length This is a mandatory field.

  2. On entering the Document Details, click [OK] to record the Condemned Expired Batch details or [Reset] to clear the entered data.

  3. Enter the details in the mandatory fields. <Related Field Description>

  4. Click [Search] to display the Item details.

  5. Select the check box against the Item to eliminate the expired/non-expiry items/batches. The details displayed are Item, Store, Expiry Date, Batch ID, Trade Name, Bin Location and Quantity on Hand.

  6. Select the ‘Select’ check box in the heading frame to select all the displayed Items.  

You can click the information icon next to the Item for viewing the ‘Item Remarks’.

 

  1. Click [Apply] to eliminate the condemned batches or [Reset] to clear the entered data.

  2. On applying, the Confirm Finalize popup is displayed to condemn the selected batches.

  3. Click Yes to finalize the condemned items/batches.

  4. The system verifies whether the remarks are recorded at the document level. If you choose to generate the report prior to finalization, the report is generated with the label ‘Proposed’. The report is generated with the label ‘Finalized’ if generated after finalization of the condemn transaction.

  5. Enter the Remarks.

If you don’t enter the remarks, an alert message ‘Please enter the Condemn Doc Remarks to proceed’ is displayed. Upon acknowledging this remark, a window to enter the condemn doc remarks is displayed. Once the Condemn remark is recorded,  you can finalize the condemn transaction.

  1. Or if you click No in the Finalize popup, the Condemn transaction remains in ‘Pending finalization’ state and a condemn Document ID is generated. The Confirm Printing popup gets displayed. Click [Yes] to print the condemned items/batches report or [No] to eliminate the condemned batches without printing the report.

The selected batches will be marked as ‘Suspended’. The same will be indicated in the Batch search window during transactions. Processing of Suspended batches is controlled based on the User Access rights, by not displaying these batches in the Batch search window. Finalization of Condemn transaction will not be allowed if any of the items within the condemn transaction are pending for Finalization under other transactions.

  1. If you finalize the condemn document without entering the batch-wise remarks or document-level remarks, an error message ‘It is mandatory to record remarks to finalize condemning the batches’ is displayed in a popup. You can edit the remarks whenever you want. When you click ‘Yes’, the Modify Document Details popup is displayed to enable you to record the mandatory remarks.

  2. Click [Query] to invoke the Query screen. To finalize the Condemn transactions at a later point, enter the search criteria and search for documents pending finalization of condemning.

  3. The From and To dates are defaulted based on the ‘number of days duration’ specified in the Facility Parameter config. The ‘From’ and ‘To’ Dates must be less than or equal to current date with To date being always greater than or equal to From date.  The Doc Type drop-down list defaults to ‘Select’ if there are multiple values and Condemn ID search criteria is blank by default and allows 10 characters in length. Both are in enabled state by default.

  4. Click Search; the documents pending for condemning are displayed.

  5. Click the Doc Type hyperlink to open the document; the document details are displayed.

  6. The Proposed Condemn Quantity column displays the Condemn qty for finalization.

  7. A new column header labelled Available for Condemn is added adjacent to Proposed Condemn Quantity and indicates the quantity available for condemn. The quantity in Available for Condemn differs from Proposed Condemn Quantity if any transactions are done on the items marked for condemn, before finalizing the condemn transaction.

  8. Unselect any items that you do not want to condemn and click Apply to finalize the condemn transaction. All such items are excluded from finalization.  

  9. Select the documents and click Delete, if you want to delete a complete document without finalizing it. A confirmation popup message ‘Delete All Items of the Document?’ is displayed. Click Yes to delete.
