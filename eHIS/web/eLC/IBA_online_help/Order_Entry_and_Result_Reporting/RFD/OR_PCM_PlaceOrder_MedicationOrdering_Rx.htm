Rx Tab

Using this option, Practitioner places a normal medical prescription recording the Dosage and Frequency details.

 

Placing RX Orders

 

  1. Click [Rx] to place Normal orders.

  2. Click [Current Rx] to view the medications prescribed and currently under usage.

  3. The barcode of the scanned item is displayed in Barcode field after scanning, if applicable.

Once the item is scanned the Barcode field gets disabled. After Confirm/Cancel button is clicked, the Barcode field gets blank and enabled.

If the item is confirmed, the item cannot be added twice.

  1. Click Allergies to view the Allergy List screen.

  2. Click [Drug Profile] to view the Drug Profile of the selected patient.

    The Patient Drug Profile screen gets displayed with the active medication orders.

     

    You can click the Drug Profile/ External Drug Profile/Medical Item tabs to view the relevant details. Else you can enter the search criteria and click [Search] to view the patient drug details.

    When you select the Drug radio button and click Search, the Drug Profile tab is selected.

     

    The practitioner can view complete information on Drugs prescribed, Prescribed quantity, Dispensing status, Dispensing quantity, Prescribing practitioner, Ordering location and others under drug profile. Option to view the profile based on patient class is provided. In addition to viewing the profile, option to cancel or discontinue drug based on status is provided.

    You can view the orders with the Status: All, Active, Completed, Future Rx, Discontinued, On Hold, Returned and Cancelled.

     

    Click Remarks hyperlink to view the Drug remarks. Click [Close] to exit from the remarks screen. Click Dispensed Locns hyperlink to view the Dispensed Location details.

     

    If you select Medical Item radio-button and click [Search], the Medical Item tab is selected. You must enter the mandatory search criteria such as Item Name, From Date and To Date. The other criteria are not mandatory.

     

    You can now view the status of the ordered Medical Items in the Medical Item tab within the patient’s Drug Profile screen instead of checking the status of the ordered Medical Item in the Order Tracking screen within the Clinician Access module.

     

    The Medical Item tab displays the Medical item details such as Item Name, Status of the Medical Item, Patient Class, Ordered Date, Order Quantity, Dispense Quantity, Ordered By and Ordered Details.

     

    You can click the Dispense Quantity hyperlink to view additional details such as Dispense Date, Dispense Item, Dispense Location, Dispense Quantity, Dispense By and Dispense Status of the Medical Item in the Dispense Location dialog.

  3. Enter the Drug details in the mandatory (*) fields. <Related Field Description>

  4. Click [Schedule] to record the Schedule Frequency.

    The pre-defined schedules for the selected frequency are displayed by the system, by default. The unit doses displayed corresponds to dose defined as strength or by quantity. The practitioner can modify only the time component. After specifying the required time frames, click [OK] to confirm. The modified schedule can be viewed by moving the mouse over the ‘Scheduled Dose’ hyperlink.

  5. Click [Refill] to enter the Refill details.

    Enter the following refill details:

    1. No of Refill(s) – Enter the number of times the selected drug needs to be refilled in numeric format of three digits maximum length. The entered value must be within the refill limits specified in PH parameter for facility. Based on the number of refills, the refill period details are displayed as shown below. The start and end date are displayed for each refill. The refill period is displayed as read-only text.

    2. Start Date – The refill medication start date and time is displayed. The first refill start date and time begins on the end date and time of original prescription and subsequent refill(s) start date and time begins on the end date and time of previous refill.

    3. End Date - The refill medication end date and time is displayed. The end date and time is calculated by adding the specified duration to the start date and time of the refill.

  6. Click Drug Remarks hyperlink to enter the additional information with regards to the selected Drug.

    Select the Prescription Remarks from the list. The Prescription Remarks associated to the selected to the Drug is displayed in the list.

    Click [OK] to record the Drug Remarks or [Cancel] to exit from the Order Format screen.

  7. Click [Override] to enter the override remarks if the selected Drug is a Duplicate Drug, Exceeds dose limit or Allergic to the patient.

    • Enter the Remarks in alphanumeric format of 255 characters maximum length. This is a mandatory field.

    • Click the respective hyperlink to view the Duplicate Drug, Exceed Dose Limit or Allergic details.

    • Click [OK] to record the Override remarks.

  8. You can select the check box to override the Duplicate Drug/Exceed Dose Limit/Allergic Drugs.

  9. Click Drug Indications hyperlink, the drug indications entered in the PH Drug master is displayed by default. The drug indications can be modified if required. On updating the drug indications, click [OK].

  10. Click [Medical Item] to invoke the Medical Items screen.

    Enter the Medical Item details:

    1. Enter the Item Name in alphanumeric format of 60 characters maximum length or select the required Item name using [?] function.

    2. On selecting the Item, the Trade Names associated to the Item are displayed. Select the required Trade Name from the list. The selected Item details are displayed in the Selected Item frame.

    3. Select the check box to include the Item to record the prescription.

    4. Click ‘Item Description’ hyperlink to modify the Item details.

    5. Click [Confirm] to add the Item details or [Clear] to clear the entered data.

    6. Click [Release Rx] to release the order or [Cancel] to cancel the order.

  11. Click [Previous Orders] to view the Previous Order details.

    The practitioner is provided with the option of viewing order placed previously. Previous orders can also be copied to the existing prescription. Active orders are not listed as previous order. Only expired, cancelled, discontinued and completed orders are listed.

     

    Specify the following query criteria to get the previous orders:

    1. Patient Class – Select the required class from the list. The listed values are Both, Inpatient and Outpatient.

    2. Orders – Select the option as either Self Related or All Practitioners. If ‘Self Related’ is selected, then all previous orders placed by the prescribing practitioner are listed. If ‘All Practitioners’ is selected, then orders placed by all the practitioners are listed.

    3. Order status - Select the order status from the list. The listed values are All, Cancelled, Discontinued, and Completed.

    4. Group By – Select the option as either Medication Orders or Drug. If the selected option is ‘medication Orders’, then drugs are grouped under their respective order numbers. If the selected option is ‘Drug’, then prescribed drugs are grouped alphabetically.

    5. Patient ID - The selected Patient ID is displayed as read-only text.

    6. Order Date [From-To] - The order period displayed is based on the period specified in 'PH Parameter for Facility' function.  Specify the required period in date format of dd/mm/yyyy or click [?] function to select the [From - To] Order Date. These fields are mandatory.

    On entering the required details click [Search] to invoke the search process. The following details are displayed.

    1. Select – Select the check box to select the drug to be copied for the current prescription. On checking the check box, the drug(s) under the order are selected. Order date/time, ordering practitioner’s ID and ordering location are displayed as header contents.

    2. Status Indicator – The following indicators are displayed based on the status.

    Click [Preview] to view the selected Order, [Copy] to copy the selected Order, [Close] to exit from the Previous Orders screen.

  12. Click [Confirm] to confirm the selected prescription, the interaction details between the interacting drugs are displayed in the alert message. The same alert message is displayed when Release Rx button is clicked without clicking the Confirm button. Additionally, the selected dosage form is considered while checking the interaction between the drugs ordered.

  13. For Severity level set as ‘Major’ in the Drug Interaction screen, the font color in the alert message will be red; whereas the font color for all other severities will be blue. The interacting description as set in the Drug Interaction screen is displayed in the alert message.

  14. Click [Cancel] to clear the entered details, [Reset] to revert to original default values, [Clear] to clear the entered details.

  15. On clicking [Confirm], a Duplicate Drug Alert gets displayed if the ordered drug is already ordered in the previous prescription in the ‘Existing Order’ tab. You can cancel or discontinue the duplicate drug. This functionality is applicable only if the ‘Discontinue/Cancel Duplicate Medication in Normal Rx’ check box is selected in the PH Parameter for Facility function.

    • In the alert message, click [Yes], the ‘Discontinue/Cancel Medication’ dialog gets displayed. This dialog displays the list of all duplicate drugs at the same time. The current drug and the duplicate drugs are grouped based on Generic ID. Each Generic ID and the Name will be displayed first, then the current drug belonging to the Generic Name is displayed in header and the corresponding duplicate drugs will be displayed below.

    • The duplicate drugs will have either the Discontinue or Cancel check box. The Discontinue or Cancel check box will be selected or cleared by default based on the selection of Default Select Discontinue/Cancel Duplicate Medication check box.

    • If this configuration is not done, to cancel or discontinue the duplicate order, select the check box under ‘Perform’ column for the respective order. Cancel check box is displayed for pending orders. While Duplicate check box is displayed for orders other than pending status.

    • On selecting any one of the check boxes, or both the ‘Cancel’ and ‘Duplicate’ check boxes, a new ‘Reason’ dialog is displayed.

    • Select the required reason for cancelling the order and click [Record].

    • The ‘Operation Completed Successfully’ message gets displayed. Click [OK], the ‘Discontinue/Cancel Medication’ dialog gets closed.

    • If the doctor wants to open the ‘Discontinue/Cancel Medication’ dialog again, click [Discontinue/Cancel Orders] provided in the Pharmacy Orders screen. This button will appear only if there is a duplicate drug for the patient.

  16. On confirming the prescription details, the details are displayed in the Controlled frame in the left hand side.

  17. Click Drug name hyperlink to modify the prescription details.

  18. The Drug Information icon is displayed if the selected Drug is associated with the External Drug Code. The External Drug Code is linked with the Drug while creating the Drug in Drug function of Pharmacy Management module.

  19. The Indicators are displayed with unique color code against to prescribed Drug.

    Appearance of color-coded indicators against certain drugs denoted the following:

     

    Indicator

    Description

    Allergic –

    The icon indicates the drug as allergic. If the patient is allergic to the selected drug, then this icon displays. The practitioner can prescribe the drug after entering a valid overriding reason.

     

    On clicking the ‘Allergic’ icon, the ‘Allergy Indications’ window displays. The allergy description includes Generic Name, Reaction Details and Severity.

     

    If a different drug with same Generic Name is selected for the patient without record of Adverse Event, the Allergy Indications dialog is displayed since the drug name is different although the Generic Name is same.

     

    If you click the Close button, you must enter the override reason.  

     

    Click [More Details] to view more details about the allergies recorded for the patient. The Allergy List screen gets displayed.

    Beyond Drug Dosage Limit -

    This icon indicates exceeding of drug dosage limit of the selected drug. Dosage limit of each drug can be set optionally. The set limit can be specific to age group and gender. Unit and daily dosage limits can be set for the drug. If the prescribed drug exceeds the set limit, then this icon displays against the drug. But, the practitioner can override the same by specifying valid reason.

    Duplicate Drug -

    This icon displays on the extreme right side of the prescription pad. It indicates drug duplication. Duplication of drug is checked based on generic name. If the selected drug is already a part of patient’s active drug profile, then the prescribing practitioner can override the same by specifying valid reason. If the selected drug falls beyond the active drug profile period, then drug can be prescribed without specifying the reason.

     

    Click on ‘Duplicate Drug’ hyperlink to view the duplicate medication details.

    External Database Checks -

    This icon indicates the External Database Checks depending on the checks enabled in the Admin Setups. If the patient is prescribed heavy dose, or the prescribed drug is allergic or a duplicate drug, then this icon displays with a Medication Alert. The prescribing Practitioner can prescribe the drug after entering a valid overriding reason.

    Drug Interaction -

    This icon indicates drug interaction.  

     

    On clicking this icon, a ‘Drug Interaction Details’ dialog is displayed. The drug interaction details include Generic Name/Drug Name of the drugs, Severity Level, Significant Level, Probability, Restrict Transaction, and Interaction Description.  

     

    The prescribing Practitioner can prescribe the interaction drug after entering a valid overriding reason.

    This is applicable only for Normal Rx.

    Override reason has to be entered for outpatient to prescribe a duplicate drug. But for inpatients and emergency patients, duplicate drug can be prescribed only for STAT frequency.

  20. Click the pink icon to invoke the Drug Interaction screen. You can check the interaction details and dosage form details in this screen. This pink icon will appear if the interaction exists between the drugs that are ordered.

  21. Click [Preview Rx] to view the prescription details before releasing the order. The Patient details and the Current Prescription details are displayed as read-only text. The font and background color of the Route value is based on the setup done in Admin Route.

  22. Click [Release Rx] to release the prescribed prescription.

  23. Click [Preview & Release Rx] to preview of prescription mandatory for All Patients. The system will show only one button “Preview & Release Rx” in place pharmacy Order Screen. This button is displayed only if you select the checkbox Prescription Preview Mandatory in PH Parameter for facility screen.

On click of Release Rx, the Print Option dialog is displayed with a new option ‘MAR Label’ selected, by default.  

  1. You are prompted with a message to print the prescription on releasing. Click [OK] to print the prescription or [Cancel] to cancel the printing.

  2. On releasing the prescription, a unique Order ID is generated. Order details can be viewed under ‘Existing Orders’ function.

If ‘Print Refill Prescriptions along with Main’ is checked in PH Parameter for Facility, then Refill prescriptions are printed along with the main prescription.

If the ‘Restrict Transaction’ check box is selected in Drug Interaction in PH Code Setup  and order for an interaction drug is raised, an alert message is prompted on clicking Confirm or Release Rx buttons. Thereby, the interaction drugs are not dispensed to the patient.

If the ‘Restrict Transaction’ check box is cleared, order for an interaction drug can be raised and the drugs are dispensed.
