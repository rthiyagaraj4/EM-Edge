Compounding Rx

The compounding Rx helps the practitioner, to place an order for a mixture of Drugs. The selection of minimum of two Drugs is mandatory. The ‘Used for Extemporaneous Preparation’ check box must be selected for the Compounding Drug under ‘Characters’ tab in the Drug function of Pharmacy Management module to populate the Drug for compounding.

 

Placing Compounding Orders

 

  1. Click [Compounding] to place an order for a mixture of Drugs.

  2. Click [Current Rx] to view the medications prescribed and currently under usage.

  3. Click [Drug Profile] to view the Drug Profile of the selected patient.

    The ‘Patient Drug Profile’ screen gets displayed with the active medication orders. The practitioner can view complete information on Drugs prescribed, Prescribed quantity, Dispensing status, Dispensing quantity, Prescribing practitioner, Ordering location and others under drug profile. Option to view the profile based on patient class is provided. In addition to viewing the profile, option to cancel or discontinue drug based on status is provided.

    You can view the orders with the Status: All, Active, Completed, Future Rx, Discontinued, On Hold, Returned and Cancelled.

    Click Remarks hyperlink to view the Drug remarks. Click [Close] to exit from the remarks screen. Click Dispensed Locns hyperlink to view the Dispensed Location details.

  4. Enter the Drug details in the mandatory (*) fields. <Related Field Description>

  5. Click Remarks hyperlink to enter the additional information with regards to the selected Drug.

  6. Click [Override] to enter the override remarks if the selected Drug is a Duplicate Drug, Exceeds dose limit or Allergic to the patient. The [Override] will appear only if drug is ‘Duplicate’, ‘Allergic ‘or ‘Beyond Dose Limit’.

    • Enter the Remarks in alphanumeric format of 255 characters maximum length. This is a mandatory field.

    • Click the respective hyperlink to view the Duplicate Drug, Exceed Dose Limit or Allergic details.

    • Click [OK] to record the Override remarks.

     

  7. Click [Schedule] to record the Schedule Frequency.

    The pre-defined schedules for the selected frequency are displayed by the system, by default. The unit doses displayed corresponds to dose defined as strength or by quantity. The practitioner can modify only the time component. After specifying the required time frames, click [OK] to confirm. The modified schedule can be viewed by moving the mouse over the ‘Scheduled Dose’ hyperlink.

  8. You can select the check box to override the Duplicate Drug/Exceed Dose Limit/Allergic Drugs.

  9. Click [Release Rx] to release the prescribed prescription or [Reset] to clear the entered details.

  10. You are prompted with a message to print the prescription on releasing. Click [OK] to print the prescription or [Cancel] to cancel the printing.

  11. On releasing the prescription, a unique Order ID is generated. Order details can be viewed under ‘Existing Orders’ function.
