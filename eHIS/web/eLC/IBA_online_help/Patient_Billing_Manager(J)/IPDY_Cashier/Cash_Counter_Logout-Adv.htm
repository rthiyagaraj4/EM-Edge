Cash Counter Logout – Advance

From this function you can Logout of the Inpatient or Daycare patient Cash Counter at the end of the shift. You can also View the Cash Collection report and the Cashier journal for the day.

Cash Counter Logout - Advance function is used to logout from the Inpatient or the Daycare Cash Counter at the end of the shift. A subsequent Cashier cannot login to Cash Counter at the beginning of the next shift, if the preceding Cash Counter User does not logout of the Cash Counter at the end of the shift. Cashier is able to view the Settlement Type, the total amount cancelled, receipt amount, actual amount and the discrepancy amount. The opening balance at the beginning of the shift and the closing balance at the end of the shift are also displayed. Cashier has to logout of the Cash Counter and re-login when the shift has exhausted. A report is generated on clicking [Close Cash Counter] displaying the bill collection and the deposit collection details. A Senior Cashier overseeing the Cash Counter transactions and the Cashier handing the transaction report at the end of the shift has to sign off on the report generated, to confirm the Cash Counter transactions at the end of the day.  

Logout from the Inpatient/Daycare Cash Counter

From the Transactions > IP/DY Cashier, select Cash Counter Logout – Advance Function.

The Cash Counter Logout – Advance screen gets displayed.

  1. Select a Cash Counter from the list. The Cash Counters defined using the Cash Counter function gets listed. The name of the Cashier who has logged-in to the Cash Counter gets displayed along with the login date and time.

  2. The current date and time is displayed as the Closing Date and Time.

  3. The settlement-wise collection details for the respective Cash Counter shift gets listed.

    1. Settlement Type – The Settlement Type using which the payment was made at the Cash Counter gets displayed. The Code and Description of the respective Cash Counter gets displayed.

    2. Total Count – The Total Count indicates the number of transactions at the Cash Counter for the respective Settlement Type.

    3. Cancelled Count - The Cancelled Count indicates the number of transactions for the respective Settlement Type which were cancelled at the Cash Counter.

    4. Bounced Count – The Bounced Count indicates the number of cheque transactions which were considered invalid by the Bank due to several reasons.

    5. Cancel Amount - The total amount cancelled by the Cashier in the Cash Counter gets displayed.

    6. Receipt Amount – The receipt amount indicates the total amount for all the receipts generated in the Cash Counter.

    7. Actual Amount – The actual amount indicates the definite amount for all the transactions in the Cash Counter including the receipt amount and the cancelled amount. It is the total amount settled at the Cash Counter while logging out from the shift.

    8. Discrepancy Amount – The discrepancy amount indicates that the difference in the actual amount.

  4. The settlement details during the closing of the shift get displayed.  

    1. Self Pay Bill Amount – The self pay bill amount indicates the amount settled by the patient. The ‘Nos’ adjacent to the text box indicates the number of transactions where the patients’ have settled the bill amount.

    2. Customer Payable Amount - The amount settled by the Customer gets displayed. The ‘Nos’ adjacent to the text box indicates the number of transactions where the Customers’ have settled the bill amount.

    3. Cash Opening Balance – The amount entered while logging into the Cash Counter gets displayed as the Cash Opening Balance.

    4. Cash Closing Balance – The total amount collected at the Cash Counter for the respective shift gets displayed.

  5. Click [View Cashier Journal] to view the cashier report  for the day.

    1. Receipt Time – The date and time when the receipt was generated gets displayed.

    2. Cash Counter – The Cash Counter code gets displayed.

    3. Shift – The functioning shift number gets displayed.

    4. Document Type – The Document Type against which the payment was collected during the Cash Counter transaction gets displayed.

    5. Document Number – The Document Number against which the payment was collected during the Cash Counter transaction gets displayed.

    6. Document Amount – The amount settled against the respective document number and the type gets listed.

    7. Type – This indicates the Receipt Type selected while generating the receipt.

    8. Nature – The Receipt Nature selected while generating the receipt gets displayed.

    9. Settlement – The Settlement Type selected to clear the payment in the Cash Counter gets displayed.

  6. Click [Cancel] to cancel the transaction.

  7. Click [Close Cash Counter] to logout from the Cash Counter.

You cannot logout of the Cash Counter, if there exists any discrepancy in the settlement amount.

If the Cashier has already logged into one of the Cash Counters, a message is displayed indicating that the Cashier has already logged into the Cash Counter.
