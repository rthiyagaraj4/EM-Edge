Check-in Accompanying Person

From this function you can Check-in an accompanying person for an Inpatient.

An accompanying person has to be associated with an Inpatient. An accompanying person is the one who helps the Inpatient.

Checking-in an Accompanying Person

From the IP Transactions > Lodger/Accompanying Person, select the Check-in Accompanying Person function.

The Check-in Accompanying Person screen gets displayed.

  1. Click [Search] to display the list of all Inpatients or enter the search criteria and click [Search] to select specific Inpatients. Click [Clear] to reset the records displayed in the result area.

    Enter the following query criteria to select the required patient record:

    1. Nursing Unit – Select the Nursing Unit using [?] function. The values listed belong to the Operator Station associated to the logged in user.

    2. Admission Date From-To – Enter the Admission Date range in dd/mm/yyyy format or select the date from the calendar function.

    3. Specialty – Select the Specialty of the Nursing Unit by clicking on [?] function.

    4. Practitioner – Select the Practitioner by clicking on [?] function.

    5. Encounter ID – Enter the Encounter ID in numeric format of 12 digits maximum length.

    6. Patient ID - Enter the Patient ID in alphanumeric format of maximum length as specified in MP Parameter or select the ID using [?] function.

  2. On searching, Inpatient Details are displayed based on the entered criteria.

  3. Click ‘+’ hyperlink to view the Current Encounter details.

  4. Click the ‘Encounter ID’ hyperlink to display the Check-in Accompanying Person screen.

  5. Enter the Accompanying Person Details and Stay Details in the mandatory (*) fields.

    1. Period From – Enter the check-in time of the Accompanying Person in date format. The current system date and time is displayed, by default. This field is mandatory.

    2. Period To – Enter the period until when the Accompanying Person stays with the patient in the facility. This field is mandatory.

    3. Nursing Unit – Select the Nursing Unit using [?] function.

    4. Authorized By – Enter the person who authorized the check-in of the Accompanying Person in alphanumeric format of 200 characters maximum length. This field is mandatory.

    5. Remarks – Enter the Remarks (if any) regarding checking-in the accompanying person in alphanumeric format of 200 characters maximum length.

    Enter the following details to check-in the accompanying person.

    1. Patient ID – Enter the Patient ID in alphanumeric format of 10 characters maximum length or select the ID using [?] function. On selecting the Patient ID, Name, Other Alternate ID's, Gender, Age and Contact Details of the selected patient are displayed as read-only text. This field is mandatory.

    2. Citizen ID – Enter the Citizen ID and other Alternate ID's in the format as specified.

    3. Prefix – Select the Prefix from the list. The list of pre-defined prefixes is listed in the list.

    4. First Name – Enter the First Name of the Accompanying Person in alphanumeric format of maximum length as specified in MP Parameter.

    5. Second Name – Enter the Second Name of the Accompanying Person in alphanumeric format of   maximum length as specified in MP Parameter.

    6. Third Name – Enter the Third Name of the Accompanying Person in alphanumeric format of   maximum length as specified in MP Parameter.

    7. Family Name – Enter the Family Name of the Accompanying Person in alphanumeric format of maximum length as specified in MP Parameter.

    8. Suffix – Select the suffix from the list. The list of pre-defined suffix is listed in the list.

    9. Gender – Select the Gender from the list. The listed values are Male, Female and Unknown. This is a mandatory field.

    10. Age – Enter the Age in numeric format of three digits maximum length for years, two digits maximum length for months and two digits maximum length for days. If ID is entered, Age is displayed as read-only text. The months must not exceed 11 months and days must not exceed 31 days.

    11. Relationship with Patient – Select the Relationship of the accompanying person with the patient from the list.

    The fields and their order displayed in the screen may vary depending on the MP Parameter Setup.

    On clicking [Contact Details], a contact details screen displays to enter the address of the person. Once the details are entered, click [Close] to close the screen. The control shifts back to the Check-in Accompanying Person screen.

  6. Click [Record] to check-in the accompanying person or [Close] to exit from screen.

  7. If internal reports are defined, the Internal Reports screen gets displayed. Click [OK] to generate Internal Reports or [Cancel] to exit from the screen.

 

You can check-in an accompanying person for an Inpatient based on the IP Parameter Setup.
