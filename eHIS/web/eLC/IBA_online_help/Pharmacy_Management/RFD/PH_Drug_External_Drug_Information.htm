External Drug Information

The system displays the information of a particular drug and alerts for Overdose, Duplicate Therapy, Drug Interaction, Contraindication, and Allergy based on the defined parameters in the “PH Parameter for Facility” function. The information of the selected drug like ‘Drug Classification’, ‘Common Orders’, Drug Interaction’, ‘Contraindications’, ‘Side Effects’, ‘Precautions’, ‘Over Dose’, ‘Drug-Food Interactions’ and ‘Patient Education’ gets listed in the left hand side of the Drug Information screen.

 

Click on the relevant hyperlink to view the Drug Information.

  1. Drug Classification – This function displays the AHFS (American Hospital Formulary Service) Classification and the FDB Classification of the selected drug. The AHFS Drug Classification is from the American Society of Health System. The FDB Drug Classification is from the First Data Bank. The AHFS and FDB Classification and their descriptions are displayed. You can only view the displayed details.

  2. Common Orders – This function displays the Common Orders related to the chosen drug.

  3. Drug Interaction –The effect that occurs when two or more drugs are used together is displayed in this function.

  4. Contraindications – Contraindication is a condition or factor that increases the risks involved in using a particular drug, carrying out a medical procedure or engaging in a particular activity. It is a condition, which makes a particular treatment or procedure inadvisable. For example, an untreated Pneumothorax would be an absolute contraindication to Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy. This function displays the warnings and cautions related to the chosen drug.

  5. Side Effects – Side Effects function addresses the problems of drug side effects and drug induced illness in several ways. This function alerts the Practitioner about the side effects caused by the chosen drug.

  6. Precautions – Precautions offers guidance for professionals in making appropriate decisions about drug therapy in Pediatric, Geriatric, Pregnant, or Lactating patients. This function alerts the Practitioner regarding the precautions that can avoid certain side effects on prescribing the chosen drug.

  7. Over Dose – This function alerts the Practitioner if the Drug Dose exceeds the limit.

  8. Drug – Food Interactions – This occurs when food consumed affects the ingredients in a medication being taken, preventing the medication from working the way it has to. This function alerts the Practitioner about the Drug–Food Interaction by the chosen drug. If there are no relevant Drug-Food Interaction details about the drug, “No Relevant Information Found” message is displayed.

  9. Patient-Education – This function informs the users about their medications, educating the patients about proper medication use, providing up-to-date information. This function educates the patient on the uses of the drug, how to use the drug, side effects of the drug, precautions that can be taken by the patient, Over Dose details, Notes related to the drug, and the storage details.
