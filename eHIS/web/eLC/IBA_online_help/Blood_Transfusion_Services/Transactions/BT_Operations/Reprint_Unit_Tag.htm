Reprint Unit Tag

From this function you can Re-print unit tags of a blood unit.

The Reprint Unit Tag function is used to reprint unit tags of a blood unit. The color codes defined at the bottom of the screen describes the status of the specimen selected for the processing. You can query for the units based on the specimen and select the units to re-print the tags. You can re-print the tags for blood units that are in Allocated, Crossmatched, Issued, Reverted and Returned statuses. If the Blood Group is not confirmed, a message, ‘Blood Typing is not Available’ is displayed. You can confirm a Blood Group through the ‘Confirm Blood Typing’ function. An additional field called ‘Issued By’ is added in the Component Tag and Cross-match Tag that are printed after confirming blood typing and cross-matching the blood units.

 

The color codes displayed at the bottom of the screen denotes the status of a blood unit.

– Units issued in emergency

– Autologous Unit

– Stat (to be issued immediately)

– Urgent

– Directed Unit

– Routine

– Multi allocation Unit

Reprinting Unit Tag

From the Transactions > BT Operations, double-click Reprint Unit Tag function.

The Reprint Unit Tag screen gets displayed.

  1. Click [List of Values] to select the Specimen Number to reprint the unit tag for the selected Specimen.

  2. Click [Execute Query] or press [F8] to view the patient details, product category and unit details. <Related Field Description>  

  3. Right click on the link ‘Right Click for User Option’ to select the user. The link has two pop-ups, namely, Patient Chart and Patient Specimen History. Click Patient Chart option to open the Patient Chart Screen from Clinician Access or Patient Specimen History to open the Lab screen from the Laboratory module.  

  4. Click [Details] to view the patient details.

  5. Click [Antibodies] to view the recorded antibody details of the patient. The [Antibodies] gets enabled if antibodies are recorded for the selected patient.

  6. Click [Transfusion History] to view the previous transfusion details of the patient, if any.

  7. Click [Requirements] to view the transfusion requirements recorded for the patient, if any.

  8. Click [Mother’s Specimen Details] to view the Mother’s Specimen Details. The button is displayed if the mother specimen details are recorded while requesting the blood units.

  9. Click [View XMatch Results] to invoke Cross Match Tests screen to view the cross match results. Click [OK] to close the Cross Match Tests screen.

  10. Click [View Multi-Allocation] to view the units allocated for multiple specimens. Current specimen is highlighted using color. Click [Close] to close the screen.

  11. Click [Product/Category Unit Details] to view category and product details of the unit. Click [Close] to close the screen.

  12. Select the ‘Reprint Y/N’ check box to reprint the unit tag for the selected Unit Number.

  13. Select the ‘All’ check box to reprint the unit tag for all the Unit Numbers.

  14. Click [Re-Print] to reprint the unit tag for the selected units.
